Japanese Culture

If you are working toward a career in the arts, business, education, science, or technology, understanding Japanese culture and history can benefit you. Moreover, learning to appreciate the uniqueness of another culture builds your ability to appreciate all cultures, a very important attribute in our ever-shrinking world.

Music

If you have a background in music and want to increase your knowledge and skills though majoring in another field, then the Music Minor will serve you well. The curriculum covers instrumental, vocal, theory, aural perception, and music analysis. Whether you hope to make music part of your career or just a part of your life, this minor will strike a chord with you.

Japanese Language

If you have completed your language requirements in Japanese and hope to work in Japan or with Japanese speakers in your career, the Japanese Language and Culture Minor represents a perfect complement to your major. You will achieve language competency and deeper understanding of Japanese culture that will give you an employment edge, and bring a lifetime of connection to one of our world's most influential cultures and economies.

Sociology

If you're interested in an applied-research career such as classroom teaching, business, human services, social work, gerontology, probation and law enforcement, or health, the Sociology Minor will provide you with a deeper understanding of people and their interactions. It will also provide insights that help you in your other life endeavors including raising your children and interacting in your community.

Hispanic Cultures

If you plan to pursue a career in social services, criminal justice, education, business, government, community service, advocacy, or any other area requiring regular interaction with diverse individuals, this minor will augment your major by helping you to develop important cultural understanding. Anyone living in California today will benefit from the insights gained into the Hispanic cultures and history that have so extensively shaped our state and country.

Spanish Language

If you have completed your language requirements in Spanish and plan to pursue a career that involves interaction with native Spanish speakers, the Spanish Language and Hispanic Cultures Minor represents a perfect complement to your major. Whether on the job or in your community, the ability to communicate with and appreciate the growing Latino population of California and the United States brings huge rewards. You will achieve language competency and deeper understanding of Hispanic cultures that will give you an employment edge, and bring a lifetime of connection with your neighbors.

Journalism/Media

The Journalism and Media Studies Minor allows you to explore media theory and journalism with ample opportunities for practical application. You will acquire the critical, analytical, writing, and research skills to incorporate journalistic and media-related projects into your career and life.

You will find all of the official information about the minor right here. For more detailed information including a look at student work, faculty bios, and events, please visit the Division of Humanities and Communication website.

Human Communication

By integrating traditional disciplines such as literature, philosophy, communication, and creative writing with ethnic , American, women's, cultural, and media studies, the Human Communication Minor prepares you for the diverse challenges of 21st-century life and work.
