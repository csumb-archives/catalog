Overview

The Visual & Public Art minor builds skills in painting, sculpture and photography. Using studio and community art approaches, students will address critical issues of social and cultural significance. Through the creation of individual and collaborative work, students engage in a process of ethical inquiry, self-reflection and reciprocal community service. The program builds skills in ethical judgment, social perspective, and cross-cultural empathy.

  • Read more

Visual & Public Art

Our Visual & Public Art minor helps you develop lifelong skills that bring together studio and community art approaches to artistic production, exhibition and education. You can learn painting and murals; sculpture, installation and performance art; arts education and museum studies; and photography and digital art. Come join us as we create public art that is meaningful to you, represents the hopes and dreams of local communities, and serves as a bridge between cultural and social groups.
