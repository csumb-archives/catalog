Human Communication

By integrating traditional disciplines such as literature, philosophy, communication, and creative writing with ethnic , American, women's, cultural, and media studies, the Human Communication Minor prepares you for the diverse challenges of 21st-century life and work.

You will find all of the official information about the minor right here. For more detailed information including a look at student work, faculty bios, and events, please visit the Division of Humanities and Communication website.

Creative Writing/Action

Want to acquire the creative, critical, visual, and cultural tools necessary to write poetry, fiction, creative nonfiction, and life stories? You can tailor the Creative Writing and Social Action Minor to pursue your personal interests while collaborating with community partners through service learning around community writing projects. In the process, you will advance your writing abilities, creativity, and ability to address important social issues.
