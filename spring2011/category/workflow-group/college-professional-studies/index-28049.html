Overview

The Social Work minor provides an interdisciplinary introduction to core knowledge and skills you will need to begin a professional career in social work, counseling, or case management. It also prepares students to enter a graduate Master of Social Work program. The curriculum emphasizes entry-level practice skills, professional ethics and values, human behavior and development, and an internship experience.

Overview

With a Liberal Studies B.A., you will have a solid foundation for entry into a credential program or other graduate study. Our outcomes-based, multidisciplinary major integrates the arts and humanities, natural and social sciences, and service learning. We emphasize breadth across a variety of disciplines with a subject area emphasis in at least one academic discipline.

Course Pathways

The Liberal Studies program is designed to meet California State requirements for subject matter competency for students who want to earn a multiple subjects credential to teach in K-8 schools. Taking courses across academic disciplines will prepare you to teach the subjects usually taught in elementary school and to pass the California Subject Examination Test (CSET): Multiple Subjects (required for entrance into a teacher credential program).

Advising

Faculty, staff and peer advisors work collaboratively to support and guide you through the curriculum and requirements. More specifically, faculty members review your academic plans and provide mentorship to help you position yourself to achieve your career objectives. The Liberal Studies advising coordinator, a specialist trained to advise and coordinate with you during the advising process, works one-on-one with you to develop a Major Requirements Graduation Check that outlines how you will achieve the Major Learning Outcomes.
