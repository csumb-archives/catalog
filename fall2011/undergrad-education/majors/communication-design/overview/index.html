The integrated Communication Design degree provides a broad-based understanding of current information technologies and media design. You choose and combine your studies in the areas of web design, game design, visual design, programming and scripting, and information systems to develop professional and academic skills that meet your career needs. Communication Design courses help you combine technical knowledge with design, communication, problem-solving and management skills to meet those needs. Our graduates are uniquely qualified for collaborative media projects and creative problem solving in a broad spectrum of work enrivonments, from small design firms to global high-tech corporations.

The School of Information Technology and Communication Design distinguishes itself from other university programs by its unique combination of technology, creative problem solving and design. The project-oriented and outcomes-based mode of learning has been recognized for its innovative and integrated application of technology, creativity, design, and management in student learning.

Throughout your course of study, you follow an Individual Learning Plan, culminating with a portfolio that demonstrates successful achievement of that ILP. Portfolios feature high levels of competence in communication, collaboration, technical expertise, ethical reflection, global perspective and creative problem solving skills. ITCD conducts leading-edge applied research and development in these fields providing you with hands-on, real-world experience while earning your degree. The spectrum of Communication Design students includes traditional college students, working professionals, distance learners, and industry-sponsored professionals.
