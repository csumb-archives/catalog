Introduction

California State University Monterey Bay (CSUMB) welcomes students with disabilities as members of our diverse learning community. CSUMB is committed to creating and maintaining full access to programs, activities, technologies, and facilities for all students. The CSU Chancellor’s Office designated the office for Student Disability Resources (SDR) as the authority to determine academic adjustments and other disability accommodations for eligible CSUMB students in partnership with faculty and other employees.

Student Disability Resources offers a series of programs within the Division of Student Affairs. SDR’s mission is to promote the empowerment, wellness, and full integration of student with disabilities into campus life and the campus community. Furthermore, SDR seeks to provide academic and personal support services to students with disabilities to help them attain success at CSUMB.

Student Disability Resources reccommends reasonable accommodations for enrolled or admitted students who have well documented disabilities under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973 and the Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990 (ADA). Accommodations are determined on an individual basis by Student Disability Resources through consultation with the SDR staff and the student.  In some cases, SDR may assist students with temporary disabilities.
