Foreign Language Petitions

The following outlines the Process To Petition for a Foreign Language University Learning Requirement (ULR) Alternate Pathway based on disability:

1) Schedule an appointment to meet with a Disability Resources Advisor, at Student Disability Resources (SDR). 

You will be provided with information about SDR, including all forms that must be completed to request services.  These currently include:

  • SDR Needs Assessment Form (Available in the Forms section of the SDR website)
  • Detailed documentation of your disability from a qualified professional.  If you do not have a detailed report, ask SDR for blank forms for your disability specialist or psychiatrist to complete. (More information on Disability Testing is available at http://sdr.csumb.edu/site/x7448.xml
  • SDR Information Sheet (You will fill out the Information Sheet in the SDR office.)
  • Personal/emotional effects this has had on you

2) Write or type your history of efforts to learn foreign language, including:

  • Grade School difficulties with language
  • High School or college where you attempted to learn foreign language
  • Semester and year for each course attempted
  • Course name and number for each attempt & Name of instructor
  • The outcome of each attempt (letter grade, Withdrawl, "no pass", etc.)
  • Any disability accommodations that were provided during your enrollment in the Foreign Language course; such as extended time on tests, notetakers, etc.
  • Details about learning experiences you had that focus on a particular culture.

NOTE: To help in the process, provide unofficial copies of your non-CSUMB transcripts to the Student Disability Resources office.

3) Bring your transcripts and your history of efforts to learn a foreign language to your next appointment with an SDR Advisor. 

These are all necessary in order for SDR to consider your eligibility for the petition process.

4) SDR may determine that you are eligible for the petition process based on disability. 

If so, SDR will write a letter of support for you to attach to your Petition to the Foreign Language ULR Committee to take specific culture courses in English to fulfill the Foreign Language ULR.

Determine how many culture substitution classes you need and propose Asian or Hispanic Culture classes for substitution.

5) Submit your petition and SDR letter of support to Dr. Yoshiko Saito-Abbott, Chair, University Learning Requirement for Foreign Language, Bldg. 49

6) Call the World Languages and Cultures department at (831) 582-3863 and schedule an appointment to discuss your petition with Dr. Saito-Abbott.
