How do Students with disabilities learn how to access academic and supportive programs?

The Office of Student  Disability Resources (SDR) seeks to assist staff and faculty in accommodating disabled students at CSUMB.  Accordingly, we offer a handbook intended to answer the most common questions and concerns brought to SDR.

Prospective students complete standard admissions application processes through the Office of Admissions. The disclosure of a disability on the application is not required but information about services for students with disabilities are included in application brochures and at information events.  Institutes often refer prospective students to SDR.

The CSUMB catalog includes information about SDR.   The SDR and Assistive Technology websites and FirstClass Disability Conference augment SDR orientation sessions.  These provide extensive information on University services for student with disabilities.

Persons with disabilities admitted to CSUMB are encouraged to meet with the SDR Coordinator or the Disability Resource Advisors to verify their disability and to discuss specific accommodations and adjustments for the required placement tests and projected course registration.  They are told that accommodations and academic adjustments cannot be made without following appropriate procedures.

SDR serves as an advocate for students, ensuring that they have the physical and programmatic access to all university programs that will affect their mainstreaming into the campus community. Official documentation of their disability is required to determine what kind of adaptations and accommodations may be helpful on campus.

Faculty are encouraged to discuss students' needs when they self-identify (not their diagnosis);  SDR can provide consultation and support services to  faculty as well as to students with disabilities.
