Student Responsibilities

It is the responsibility of the student to seek available assistance at CSUMB and to request specific reasonable accommodations. CSUMB encourages students to contact SDR if they think they have a disability.

To obtain Academic Accommodations or services students need to:

  1. Contact the office for Student Disability Resources.

  2. Complete the SDR Needs Assessment Form.

  3. Provide SDR with detailed documentation of your disability from a qualified physician or disability specialist. It should thoroughly explain how your disability limits your participation in courses, programs, services, jobs, activities and facilities of the University.

    SDR urges students with learning disabilities     to provide comprehensive documentation based on adult assessment instruments. Ask for SDR guidelines for documentation. SDR will file official documentation of your disability, including information about the manifestations of the disability.  SDR will ensure that disability-related documents are kept confidential.
  4. Attend a SDR Group Orientation to learn how to request disability services and accommodations at CSUMB. Dates are posted at SDR in Building 47.

  5. Meet with a SDR Disability Advisor to discuss your specific needs and determine reasonable accommodations.

  6. For each semester, complete and sumbit to SDR a Service Request Form for specific accommodations in each course, field trip, etc. Meet with a SDR Disability Advisor for review and authorization. In most cases, SDR will respond to your request within 5-10 business days of your meeting. 

    SDR will produce Course Accommodations Forms (CAF’s) authorizing your approved services and accommodations. You will submit these CAF’s to course instructors. (See "Sample Course Accommodation Form") When authorized by SDR, instructors will implement accommodations or consult SDR with any concerns or questions. Instructors are not expected to compromise essential elements of the course or evaluation standards.
  7. The Student requesting accommodations is responsible for meeting with instructors (during office hours, online, or via telephone) in a timely manner to discuss their needs and the SDR Course Accommodations Form. Request  the instructor's signature on the Course Accommodations Form as commitment to provide authorized accommodations.

    Students should notify instructors to contact Student Disability Resources if there are any concerns about providing the accommodations.

To manage disability services students need to:

  • Read and follow all steps outlined above and follow them each semester.

  • Read and follow specific service procedure outlines in the Student Disability Resources Service Request Form/Agreement for Authorized Services and other service forms.

If Alternate Formats are needed (large print, audio, electronic, braille, etc.), complete and submit a SDR Service Request Form as soon as you register for your courses. Plan ahead with your instructors, SDR and service providers.

Notify SDR immediately if:

  • Your faculty/instructor, SDR service provider, service learning community partner or other CSUMB program is not fulfilling their obligation or commitment to accommodate your disability-related needs.

  • You do not need your requested accommodations any longer.

  • You drop the course or withdraw from CSU Monterey Bay.
