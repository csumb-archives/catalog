Social Work Minor ~ Distinguishing Features

Working professionals collaborated with HHSPP to devise learning outcomes that equip our SW Minor students with the knowledge and skills that are most desired from entry-level professionals.

Students will complete a 60-hour internship with a community-based agency, providing real-world experience and opportunities to start building a professional network prior to graduation.

The Minor develops values and ethics that form the core of social workers' professional identity.

The curriculum provides options for students to take courses in human development and behavior, in addition to core social work classes.
