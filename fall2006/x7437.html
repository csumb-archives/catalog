My Body Politic

Open Forums with Simi Linton, Ph.D.
Author & Disability Rights Scholar & Activist

Open to Faculty, Students, Staff & the Community

Monday, March 27, 2006
12:00 pm - 1:30 pm-Brown Bag Luncheon
California State University Monterey Bay
6th Ave. Seaside, 93955
University Center Living Room, Bldg. 29

Session Repeated 6:00 pm - 7:30 pm
University Center Room 115, Bldg. 29

Simi Linton is one of the nationally recognized leaders in the field of Disability Studies & the Disability Rights Movement. She is the author of Claiming Disability: Knowledge and Identity and now My Body Politic, A Memoir. This is a moving autobiography contextualized by the stories of the folks she met in the disability rights movement. It includes their stories interwoven in the evolution of disabilities studies, disability rights and the disability movement.

"Witty, original, and political without being politically correct, introducing us to a cast of funny, brave, remarkable characters (including the professional dancer with one leg) who have changed the way that "walkies" understand disability. By the time Linton tells you about the first time she was dancing in her wheelchair, you will feel like dancing, too."  Carol Tavris, author of Anger: The Misunderstood Emotion

Simi will discuss Why "disability"?  Disability Rights, Disability Studies & Culture. She will also focus on integrating disability studies across the curriculum, read from her book, and exchange ideas and experiences.

Tuesday, March 28, 2006
10:00 a.m. - noon Building 18, Room 120
Join Simi in Professor John Berteaux’s Class “Ways of Knowing”

"With romance in Manhattan, a sojourn in Berkeley, classes at Columbia and wheelchair dance lessons (taught by a quadriplegic friend), Linton has succeeded in creating a life both rich and enviable. With her crackle, irreverence and intelligence, it's clear that the author would never be willing to settle. Wholly enjoyable." --Kirkus Reviews

12:15-1:30 Dining Commons Steinbeck Room, Building 16

Simi will discuss disability rights and read from her book.
