Disability-Related Complaints

“The California State University does not discriminate on the basis of disability in admission or access to, or treatment or employment in, its programs and activities. Section 504 of the Rehabilitation act of 1973, as amended, and the regulation adopted thereunder and the Americans with Disabilities Act prohibit such discrimination.” (2003-2005 CSUMB Catalog)

Students with disabilities who have concerns about equal access to any CSUMB program, service or activity, are encouraged to complete the CSUMB Reasonable Accommodation Request Form and discuss their specific concerns with the person in charge of the program, service or activity, in consultation with the Coordinator of Student Disability Resources. The CSUMB Reasonable Accommodation Request Form is available in the SDR office.

Students with disabilities who believe they have been discriminated against on the basis of their disability may file a grievance with Diversity, Equity, and Employment Practices.  For contact information, please visit their website at (http://uhr.csumb.edu/eeo/).

Any person alleging violation of rights under the ADA or Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act may also file a complaint with the appropriate Federal agency below; in general, such complaints must be filed within 180 days of alleged offense.
