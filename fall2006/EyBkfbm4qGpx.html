How can I encourage students to discuss disability-related needs?

The Office of Student  Disability Resources (SDR) seeks to assist staff and faculty in accommodating disabled students at CSUMB.  Accordingly, we offer a handbook intended to answer the most common questions and concerns brought to SDR.

The first way to encourage students to discuss disability-related accommodations with you is through the preparation of your course syllabus.  Make your syllabus available at least 3 weeks prior to the semester, with the statement below.  Second, you can create a responsive climate by announcing in several class meetings, posting on the board and the course web materials, and repeating two weeks before each assessment how students can request accommodations.

We suggest a statement inviting students with disabilities who need accommodations to make an appointment to meet with you during office hours to discuss ways to make academic adjustments and contact Student Disability Resources if they have not done so previously to verify their disability and need for accommodation. For example, “If you are a student with a disability and you need accommodations, please make an appointment with me to discuss your needs.   Bring your forms from Student Disability Resources in Building 47.”   An announcement in the first few class meetings that reinforces a welcoming atmosphere for students with disabilities is helpful. For example:        

“...Students with disabilities who require accommodations such as time extensions or test accommodations must present verification from Student Disability Resources (SDR) as soon as possible. Please schedule an appointment to discuss specifics with me. If you think a disability may impact your performance in this class, meet with SDR professional staff at Building 47 or call (831)582-3672 (Voice), or (831) 582-4024 (TTY) and see me by appointment.”
