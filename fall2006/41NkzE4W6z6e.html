What is SDR?

The Office of Student  Disability Resources (SDR) seeks to assist staff and faculty in accommodating disabled students at CSUMB.  Accordingly, we offer a handbook intended to answer the most common questions and concerns brought to SDR.

Table of Contents:

  • What is Student Disability Resources?

Student Disability Resources (SDR) promotes the empowerment, wellness and full integration of students with disabilities into campus life and the campus community.  Further, we seek to   provide academic and personal support services to students with disabilities in order for them to attain success at California State University Monterey Bay.

CSUMB utilizes a decentralized model in which students with documented disabilities participate in the academic programs and the learning community in the University with the support of SDR, faculty, staff and volunteers. 

SDR helps students and faculty in the arrangement of accommodations, advocacy, study and time management skills, academic-related counseling, and referral to on and off campus resources, and electronic/assistive technology resources and training.

CSUMB makes every effort to provide reasonable accommodations for students with disabilities, while at the same time respecting the integrity of the academic programs in which students enroll.  CSUMB does not waive (exempt) courses that are required as an essential component of any curriculum.

SDR supports the concept of self-advocacy in all students.  Requests for academic accommodations are made directly by the student and implemented by the student, SDR student assistants/service providers, and CSUMB faculty/staff.  SDR usually does not provide faculty with prior notification of a student’s intended enrollment or confidential disability information.
