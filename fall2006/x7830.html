Procedures for Requesting Alternate Formats

A student registered with Student Disability Resources with proper documentation may be eligible to receive Alternate Formats of texts or Alternate Media. Alternate media is any form of text that has been altered into electronic text (e-text) that is saved into an alternate format to be read by assistive software programs, such as Kurzweil and JAWS. Eligibility will be determined on an individual basis.

Contact the Student Disability Resources Office to schedule an appointment with an advisor if you are interested in receiving Alternate Formats of texts.

Students should follow the process below to request Alternate Formats:

  1. With a referral from an SDR Disability Advisor, schedule an appointment with the Assistive Technology Coordinator. At your appointment, you will be introduced to the software programs that are used to access Alternate Media. A recommendation will be made to SDR about what program(s)/method(s) would benefit you the most.

  2. Schedule an appointment and meet with an Alternate Media Assistant.

At your appointment, you will complete and submit:

  • The “Alternate Media Procedures and Policies” document.
  • The “Application for Alternate Media Services” form.
  • The “Electronic Text Request Documentation" form.

NOTE: You will need a syllabus for each course in which you request Alternate Formats. When the application and request is completed, the Alternate Media Assistant will determine the availability of pre-existing Electronic Texts (E-text).

If e-text from the publisher* or another source is not available you will need to consent for Alternate Format staff to create a digital format of the book. It will be scanned, proofread, and reproduced in a format for use with Kurzweil, JAWS, or an MP3 player.

The book will be re-bound and returned to you. For SDR to provide Alternate Formats of a text, you are required to provide evidence (a receipt) that you own the book and you must sign a contract about the parameters of using this service.

*If a publisher has the e-text the student must provide or sign a certified print disability statement, proof that the college or student purchased the text, proof the student is enrolled in the class, and a signed contract agreeing not to share or copy the publisher's e-text. When e-text is available and received from a publisher, it must be edited with special software. This process can take up to 3 months.
