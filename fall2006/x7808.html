Special Admissions by Exception and Appeal

California State University Monterey Bay (CSUMB) welcomes qualified applicants with disabilities.  Applicants who do not meet CSUMB Admissions criteria may appeal or request consideration of special circumstances such as disability.

To apply for admission by exception/appeal, applicants need to apply to CSUMB and:

  1. Write a letter to: Director of Admissions, CSU Monterey Bay
    100 Campus Center, Seaside CA 93955-8001

    Explain why you think you should be admitted to CSUMB, and how you think you will be successful completing CSUMB requirements (tools and strategies you have learned to use in compensation for the functional effects of your disability, how you will stay motivated as the workload and complexity of assignments is substantially greater than in high school, etc.)

  2. In your letter, authorize Admissions to consult with Student Disability Resources (SDR) about your application.  Keep a copy of your letter.

  3. Send to SDR a copy of your letter requesting special admissions, a copy of application materials, and detailed documentation of your disability diagnosis by a qualified medical doctor and/or disability specialist who is familiar with your functional limitations in an academic environment.  Contact SDR for disability documentation forms if needed.

  4. Complete and return the SDR Needs Assessment.  If you have not yet received a copy of our Needs Assessment please contact our main office at the above address/telephone number.  Give us your mailing address, or download it from sdr.csumb.edu Forms & Publications. 

Allow Admissions 6-12 weeks, after receipt of all documents, to process Special Admissions applications.  To expedite this, be sure to mail to SDR all your confidential information listed above.  Keep copies for yourself.
