Math Petitions

CSU Monterey Bay (CSUMB) is committed to making academic programs accessible to all eligible students, including those with disabilities.  Some students have particular disabilities that substantially limit their ability to do community college level math and/or university level math.

For these students, CSUMB offers a petition procedure for an alternate pathway to fulfill the Math Communication University Learning Requirement (ULR).  This procedure is consistent with the CSU Board of Trustees Executive Order 665, effective Fall 1998, to reduce remediation in Mathematics at the university level. 

Alternatives to Course Substitution

CSUMB urges prospective and enrolled CSUMB students to fulfill the Math ULR through community college remediation courses and supportive services and/or extended time to complete CSUMB Math 98/99 and other MATHCOM courses (such as STAT 200 and MATH 100).

CSUMB also urges students to use the  Academic Skills Achievement Program rather than petitioning for course substitution.  In rare disability circumstances, some students are still unable to pass these courses.

Plan Ahead 

Requests for course substitution may take six to twelve months to approve and to implement because alternate courses are often only available off campus.  When a student makes a request to the math faculty for an accommodation based on medical, psychological or other disability related needs, the student also needs to ensure that the request has been considered by the CSUMB office for Student Disability Resources (SDR).  After thorough review of related documentation and discussion with the student, SDR makes a recommendation with the student through the petition process below. 

Student confidentiality is respected at all times.  Students do not need to disclose specific diagnoses to faculty.

For CSUMB to fully consider a specific exception to policy, students need to...

 Follow these steps:

  1. Be admitted to CSUMB (Or have submitted the Special Admissions petition)
  2. Have taken the CSU Entry Level Math test (request accommodations when registering for the test)
  3. Submit to SDR your SDR Needs Assessment, current SDR Information Sheet, and your detailed draft Petition to the Math Communication University Learning Requirement Committee requesting course substitution, including your CSUMB Individualized Learning Plan if available.  Include your history of attempts to learn college level math with disability accommodations (including reduced course load). Also detail any difficulty in math in grade or high school and the emotional/personal effect this has had on you.
  4. Submit to SDR detailed disability documentation of your functional limitation in the area of learning math, by a qualified professional using adult assessments.

Disclosure of Disability Documentation

Please provide current, comprehensive disability documentation as outlined in the California State University (CSU) Guidelines for the Assessment and Verification of Students with Learning Disabilities.  These guidelines are found in pages 12 – 17 of the CSU Policy for the Provision of Accommodations and Support Services to Students with Disabilities. 

For other disabilities, forms are available from SDR for your medical doctor. (Use the Disability Documentation form for Psychological Disabilities including ADHD/ADD, or the Disability Verification form for non-psych disabilities. Both are available in the Forms & Publications section of the SDR Website. All information you submit to Student Disability Resources will be kept in confidential SDR files. Please submit all of the information listed on the front of this flyer to the Student Disability Resources office.

Submit a copy of your petition letter with:

  • Your completed Petition for Exception to University Policy form. (Also available at Admissions and Records or the Campus Service Center.
  • Detailed documentation from your medical doctor or licensed professional/practitioner that clearly states:

    Your current diagnosis, your prognosis (expected length of your functional limitations), the functional limitations of your disability diagnosis, the recommended academic accommodations based upon these functional limitations, and alternatives if your request is not practical for CSUMB to implement.
  • Schedule and keep follow up appointments (in person or telephone) with the SDR Coordinator or Disability Resource Advisors for status updates.

SDR Procedures for Requesting Accommodations

Students who request disability accommodations are responsible for following the most current SDR Procedures for Requesting Accommodations.  The procedures are available in the SDR Student Handbook.
