HPWE 100

  • Last updated in Charlotte: 03/09/06
  • Last posted to Banner: 3/13/06

Designed for freshmen and sophomore students through participation in the new student orientation called "Otter Days." Credit/no credit option. (Offered fall semester.)

HPWE 109

  • Last updated in Charlotte: 03/10/06
  • Last posted to Banner: 3/13/06

Teaches the very basic techniques, skills, and concepts of swimming to those with little or limited swimming experience.  Develop a sense of confidence in swimming through 12 steps to practice and the common combination of drills.

HPWE 110

  • Last updated in Charlotte: 03/09/06
  • Last posted to Banner: 03/13/06

Introduces the use of weight training equipment as a means of improving and maintaining muscular strength and endurance. Includes the physiological responses and adaptations of the human body to strength training, safety, risks, and benefits of strength training, muscle structure and function, and nutritional considerations. Measures progress with written self-assessment. Credit/no credit option. (Offered every semester.)

HPWE 111

  • Last updated in Charlotte: 10/25/04
  • Last posted to Banner: 10/25/04

Instructs on swim conditioning as a lifelong wellness activity. Emphasizes cardiovascular endurance and stroke efficiency. Credit/no credit option. (Offered every semester.)

HPWE 113

  • Last updated in Charlotte: 10/25/04
  • Last posted to Banner: 10/25/04

Students learn about and practice developing customized personal fitness programs using an eclectic approach to fitness. Emphasizes cardiovascular endurance through aerobic training and some strength training. Credit/no credit option. (Offered every semester.)

HPWE 114

  • Last updated in Charlotte: 03/09/06
  • Last posted to Banner: 3/13/06

Develops a consistent personal walking program to reduce stress levels and body weight , and enhance life long fitness.  Develops a regular habit of walking, addressing body metabolism, stress reduction, and endurance.  Students make a commitment to consistent periods of vigorous walking at least twice a week for approximately 40 minutes each session.  A variety of interesting walking venues are used along with special small or large group activities. Teaches stretching and warm up/cool down techniques.

HPWE 116

  • Last updated in Charlotte: 03/06/06
  • Last posted to Banner: 3/14/06

Instructs on adapting physical activity for individual needs through the use of the pool to enhance one's overall health and wellbeing. Instructor and student decide on content. In conjunction with adapted dry land, provides daily activity for individuals who are temporarily or permanently in need of adapted physical activity. Credit/no credit option. (Offered every semester.)

HPWE 117

  • Last updated in Charlotte: 10/25/04
  • Last posted to Banner: 10/25/04

This learning experience is designed for individuals who would like to select a physical activity program with adaptations to meet their individual needs. Course content is decided upon between instructor and student, resulting in many program options and variety. This experience, in conjunction with adapted aquatics can provide daily activity for individuals who are temporarily or permanently in need of adapted physical activity. Credit/no credit option. (Offered every semester.)

HPWE 118

  • Last updated in Charlotte: 03/09/06
  • Last posted to Banner: 3/14/06

A cardiovascular and muscular endurance workout.  Using flotation devices,  follows the instructor through a wide variety of movements using the water as resistance.  No experience in swimming necessary.  Good for people looking for an alternative to weight bearing exercise.

HPWE 123

HPWE 131

  • Last updated in Charlotte: 03/06/06
  • Last posted to Banner: 03/14/06

Teaches two Taiji (Tai Chi) short forms starting with a Sun Style 12 movement form. Contains qigong exercises to enhance healing and agile steps to improve mobility, especially beneficial for arthritis or beginners.  Following Development of Internal Power, a short Yang-style  energy form of Tai Chi Chi Kung I teaches the principles of body mechanics through the alignment of the bone structure with the force of gravity.  Emphasizes taoist principles and deep internal work necessary for the effective practice of any form of Taiji.  Credit/no credit option. (Offered every semester.)

HPWE 132

  • Last updated in Charlotte: 03/06/06
  • Last posted to Banner: 03/14/06

Reveals the movement of the Inner Structure of Taiji. Teaches how to move fast in five directions through Tai Chi Chi Kung II (TCCK II) fast discarge form, moving the entire body as one piece. Teaches Push Hands, energetic quality of the different directions of force, self-defense applications, and how to discharge energy from the earth through the body structure. Traditional Chen Family Taijiquan, LaoJia Yi Lu (Old Frame-First Frame) emphasizing Basic Taiji principles and theory may be offered in place of the TCCk II form. Credit/no credit option. (Offered as interest warrants.)

HPWE 410

  • Last updated in Charlotte: 10/25/04
  • Last posted to Banner: 10/25/04

An introductory course exposing students to the field of athletic training. The role of the athletic trainer is explored in relation to the physician, coach, and athlete. Emphasis is placed on prevention, recognition, and treatment of the more common sports injuries. Topics include injury management; basic evaluation procedures; head and face injuries; spinal injuries; knee injuries; and lower leg, ankle, and foot injuries. (Offered as interest warrants during spring semester.)

HPWE 461

  • Last updated in Charlotte: 03/10/06
  • Last posted to Banner: 03/14/06

Combines stress content topics, practice assignments, and support sessions. Utilizes stress research, individual inventories, perception analysis, mind re-framing, and self-monitoring techniques to manage difficult situations by utilizing stress-reduction techniques.
