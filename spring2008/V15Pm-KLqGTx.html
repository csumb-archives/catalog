Biology Minor ~ Learning Environment

It's tough to beat the Monterey Bay region and CSUMB as a place to learn about biology!

The Monterey Bay National Marine Sanctuary and the Monterey Bay Aquarium offer access to amazingly diverse marine lifeforms, from bizarre deep sea corals to blue whales.  In addition, redwood forests, rugged mountain ranges, agricultural fields, dune habitats, estuaries, vernal pools, and other habitats provide a wealth of opportunities to study an enormous variety of organisms.

Faculty collaborations and partnerships with regional research and education institutions, including local hospitals, biotech firms, wildlife research centers, and state or federal agencies involved in biological projects, give you access to a wide range of research opportunities, field trips, internships, and other unique learning experiences. 

The biology courses at CSUMB are relatively small classes with an emphasis on hands-on learning in state-of-the-art teaching labs, field trips to local habitats, and applied projects.  It's a great way to learn.
