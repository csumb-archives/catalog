Information Technology and Communication Design B.S. (formerly TMAC) ~ Overview

The integrated ITCD degree provides a broad-based understanding of current information technologies and media design. You choose and combine your studies in the areas of  software design, web design, game design, visual design, instructional design, computer networking, system administration, and information systems to develop professional and academic skills that meet your career needs. ITCD courses help you combine technical knowledge with design, communication, problem-solving, and management skills to meet those needs. ITCD graduates are uniquely qualified for collaborative projects and creative problem solving in a broad spectrum of work enrivonments, from small design firms to global high-tech corporations.

The School of Information Technology and Communication Design distinguishes itself from other university programs by its unique combination of technology, creative problem solving, and design. The project-oriented and outcomes-based mode of learning has been recognized for its innovative and integrated application of technology, creativity, design, and management in student learning.

Throughout your ITCD course of study, you follow an Individual Learning Plan, culminating with a portfolio that demonstrates successful achievement of that ILP. Portfolios feature high levels of competence in communication, collaboration, technical expertise, ethical reflection, global perspective, and creative problem solving skills. ITCD conducts leading-edge applied research and development in these fields providing you with hands-on, real-world experience while earning your degree. The spectrum of ITCD students includes traditional college students, working professionals, distance learners, and industry-sponsored professionals.
