Master of Public Policy ~ Learning Environment

You will gain experience through internships, classroom coursework, research, and online learning opportunities. You will interact with our diverse team of faculty and working policy professionals who are affiliated with institutions and agencies throughout the Central Coast.

The program offers opportunities for you to participate in challenging academic and applied policy analysis research and policy development. You will sharpen your skills through community-based practical policy development projects throughout the program, and you will use the tools, resources, and networks that result to complete a required professional internship and a graduate research project.

You may also take advantage of the educational, work, and study opportunities available through our connections with other internationally known institutions in our area, including the Monterey Institute of International Studies; University of California, Santa Cruz; and the Panetta Institute.

HHSPP campus classes are small, highly interactive, and utilize all aspects of our technologically advanced campus.
