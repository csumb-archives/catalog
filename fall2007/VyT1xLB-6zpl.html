Liberal Learning Course Descriptions

LL 300 - Junior ProSeminar - 4 credits

  • Last updated in Charlotte: 10/10/06
  • Last posted to Banner: 10/18/06

Focuses on academic orientation in Liberal Learning and advanced writing, critical thinking, collaborative learning, and research skills. Introduces philosophies underlying the liberal arts, along with a multicultural historical view of social and political philosophy. Requires junior standing; successful completion of the English Communication (ENGCOM) and ETHICS University Learning Requirements (or their General Education equivalencies in written and oral communication and critical thinking). (LL Core class)

LL 323 - World Mythological Literature - 4 credits

  • Last updated in Charlotte 3/2/07
  • Last posted to Banner 3/12/07

Surveys world mythology and global mythologies including Hindu, Norse, Celtic, and American Indian. Examines the universal motifs which make cross-cultural stories similar in light of scholarly theory. Compares mythic literature from around the world.

LL 324 - The Return of the Great Goddess - 4 credits

  • Last updated in Charlotte 10/10/06
  • Last posted to Banner 10/16/06

    • Explores the historical existence, patriarchal suppression of, and the burgeoning rebirth of this ancient, astro-biological tradition and its significance for generation equity, ecological awareness, Earth stewardship, and a cross-cultural appreciation for the important role women have played in all aspects of human history.

    LL 391 - Culture and Cultural Diversity - 4 credits

  • Last updated in Charlotte 3/2/07
  • Last posted to Banner 3/12/07

    • Develops competency in cross-cultural analysis, involving a working knowledge of one’s own ethnic heritage and the ability to make constructive comparisons with at least one other ethnic group. Addresses a conceptual understanding of culture, methodologies for studying and understanding culture, knowledge of cross-cultural relations in the U.S., socio-cultural experiences of U.S. –based social/ethnic groups and other societies, power relations and equity, and socio-political systems and effects on “status” of a cultural group. (LL Core class)

    LL 395 - The History of Science I - 4 credits

  • Last updated in Charlotte 10/10/06
  • Last posted to Banner 10/16/06

    • Traces the emergence of the scientific method and scientific modes of thought out of the ancient philosophical and religious traditions of the west from the pre-Socratic philosophers of ancient Greece to the Medieval and Renaissance “awakenings” of the seeds of modern science. Highlights the impact of ancient science on Medieval Christianity and the development of the Copernican-Newtonian worldview. (LL Core class)

    LL 395 - The Decline of Magic and the Rise of Religion - 4 credits

  • Last updated in Charlotte 10/10/06
  • Last posted to Banner 10/16/06

    • Views the end of the “Middle Ages” and the transition to the Renaissance period of rapid intellectual change and the growth of science and religion. Explores the world-view of the Great Goddess system and its contribution to the magical world, setting the stage for the rise of patriarchal religious thought and the rise of science. Links the effects of Bubonic Plague, Neo-Platonism, Alchemy, the Protestant Reformation, the Crusades, the Bible, the burning of “witches,” and the printing press to the intellectual ferment that characterized this period of European history. (LL Core class)


    LL 395 - The History of Science II - 4 credits

     

  • Last updated in Charlotte 10/10/06
  • Last posted to Banner 10/16/06

    • (Section 6) The History of Science II—The Making of Modern Science: Major trends and developments in the sciences from 17th Century to the present. Emphasizes developments with broad cultural and social implications and compares these with the increasingly important Neo-Platonic Achemical study of spirituality and healing and the contemporary Western tendency to look to and attempt to incorporate the sacred psycho-spiritual/herbal traditions of the global Indigenous systems above, during and from the Renaissance to the present quantum physics era. (LL Core class)

    LL 395 - The Introduction of Humanities - 4 credits

  • Last updated in Charlotte 10/10/06
  • Last posted to Banner 10/16/06

    • This course will be an exploration of the literature, art, architecture, music, philosophy and history of the West, with cross-cultural comparisons from the Indigenous Americas, Africa, Australia and from the ancient and early medieval world, beginning with classical Greece and Rome and the transition to the changes brought on by the Renaissance period. (LL Core class)

    LL 395 - Jungian Psychology and the Transformations of the Self - 4 credits

  • Last updated in Charlotte 10/10/06
  • Last posted to Banner 10/16/06

    • This course will take a psycho-historic look at the depth psychological approaches of Carl Gustav Jung and his many followers, including such neo-Jungians as Jean Shinoda-Bolen, Marian Woodman, Robert Johnson, and many others. The archetypal psychological approach for solving everyday individual human problems, as well as the world problems like hunger, poverty, war, love, and encroaching environmental problems. A dream journal will be one of the personal activities that will encourage each person to apply what they have learned.

    LL 395 - Classical Mythology - 4 credits

  • Last updated in Charlotte 10/10/06
  • Last posted to Banner 10/16/06

    • This course surveys the classical myths of Greek and Roman Mythology and their influence on later literature, law, art, science, medicine, politics, and various social issues. As the major mythological underpinnings of Western thought, Classical Mythology will aid our students in any of their current careers.

    LL 400 - Senior Capstone - 4 credits

  • Last updated in Charlotte 10/10/06
  • Last posted to Banner 10/16/06

    • Senior Capstone: The culminating learning experience in the major requires students to demonstrate depth knowledge, and integration and synthesis of their learning across the disciplines through a well-defined research paper and project. Aligns with LS MLOs 1-10. Remaining units at point of Capstone must be no more than 14 semester units (including the 4 unit capstone). (LL Core class) (Offered every semester.)
