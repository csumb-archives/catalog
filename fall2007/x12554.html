Liberal Studies B.A. Teaching Pathway ~ Typical Course Pathways

The Liberal Studies program is designed to meet California State requirements for subject matter competency for students desiring to attain a multiple subjects credential to teach in K-8 schools. You take sequences of courses across academic disciplines that prepare you to teach the subjects usually taught in elementary school and to pass the California Subject Examination Test: Multiple Subjects (required for entrance into a teaching credential program).

Collaboratively with an advisor, you will design an Individual Learning Plan to achieve your personal educational and career outcomes while also satisfying State requirements for teaching preparation, University Learning Requirements, Graduation Learning Outcomes, and Major Learning Outcomes. Given that, you should follow the pathways below:

General Freshman Pathway

General Transfer Pathway

These pathways serve as tools for timely progress toward the degree. Before enrolling, you can use them to guide your schedule in this program. After enrolling, you and your advisors can use them as a starting point for building your personalized outcomes-based ILP.
