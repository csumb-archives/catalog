Business Administration, B.S. ~ Distinguishing Features

Hands-on field-based learning including internships and service work in businesses/corporations, government agencies, and nonprofit organizations

Close faculty-student contact including one-on-one mentoring, and coordinated interaction with student peers and business and community leaders

Access to and learning from a diverse student body that mirrors the multicultural workplace of the 21st century

Critical thinking, ethics, and entrepreneurial and international perspectives that help you to make things happen in business in the face of rapid change

Collaboration with other majors, allowing you to concurrently acquire skills in many other academic fields while studying business administration
