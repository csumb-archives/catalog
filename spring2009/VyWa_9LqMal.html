Information Technology and Communication Design B.S. ~ Typical Course Pathways

Because we have built our academic programs on an outcomes-based education model, you have lots of latitude to design an Individual Learning Plan to achieve your personal target outcomes while also satisfying University Learning Requirements, Graduation Learning Outcomes and Major Learning Outcomes.

These pathways serve as recommendations, not prescribed programs of study. Before enrolling, you can use them to get a feel for what you can do in this program. After enrolling, you and your advisors can use them as a starting point for building your personalized outcomes-based ILP.

If you have any questions regarding the ITCD program, feel free to email the ITCD Peer Advisor at itcd_advisor@csumb.edu or visit the Peer Advising office in Building 18, Room 150.

Lower Division Pathway For Students Entering CSUMB as a Freshman

If you enter CSUMB as a freshman preparing for the Information Technology and Communication Design major, the following would be a typical pathway that you might follow to complete the requirements of this major.

* Assumes preparation for Pre-Calculus completed in High School

Freshmen Semester 1 - Fall
(15 units)

FYS 100-T ~ First Year Seminar-Technology ~ 6 units
Satisfies the Tech/Info GLO

CST 211 ~ Technical Communications ~ 4 units
Satisfies the Eng/Com A ULR

SL 200S/LS* ~ Intro to Service ~ 4 units
*Any course that satisfies the Community Participation ULR

Any 1 or 2 unit KIN activity course* ~ 1 unit
*Any course that satisfies the Vibrancy Activity ULR

Freshmen Semester 2 - Spring
(15 units)

CST 271 ~ Digital Culture ~ 4 units
Satisfies the Lit/Pop Culture ULR

CST 212 ~ Technical Presentations ~ 4 units
Satisfies the Eng/Com B ULR

CST 201/201L ~ Media Tools ~ 4 units
Satisfies the Creative/Artistic Expression ULR; Satisfies the Interaction Design MLO

Vibrancy Theory* ~ Foundations of Wellness ~ 3 units
*Any course that satisfies the Vibrancy Theory ULR

Sophomore Semester 1 - Fall
(16 units)

GS 200 ~ Politics of Everyday Life ~ 4 units
Satisfies the Democratic Participation ULR; Satisfies the U.S. Histories ULR

ESSP 201/201L* ~ The Nature of Science ~ 4 units
*Any course that satisfies the Science Content ULR

MATH 130 ~ Pre-Calculus ~ 4 units plus MATH 130L - 1 unit
or MATH 115 ~ Finite Mathematics ~ 4 units
Satisfies the Math ULR, Partially satisfies the Mathematics for Computing MLO

Foreign Language 101* ~ (*Any language) ~ 4 units
Partially satisfies the Language GLO

Sophomore Semester 2 - Spring
(16 units)

BIO 204* ~ Intro to Life Science ~ 4 units
*Any course that satisfies the Science Methods ULR

CST 231/231L ~ Problem Solving and Programming with C++ ~ 4 units
or CST 221 ~ Scripting for Multimedia ~ 4 units
Partially satisfies the Programming and Problem Solving MLO

MATH 170 ~ Discrete Mathematics ~ 4 units
or MATH 116 ~ Finite Mathematics II~ 4 units
Satisfies the Math ULR, Partially satisfies the Mathematics for Computing MLO

Foreign Language 102* ~ (*Any language) ~ 4 units
Partially satisfies the Language GLO
