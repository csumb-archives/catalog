Collaborative Health and Human Services B.A. ~ General Junior Transfer Pathway

Because CSUMB is outcomes-based, this chronological pathway lists each target outcome first either as an MLO (Major Learning Outcome) or ULR/GLO (University Learning Requirement / Graduation Learning Outcome). It then shows courses aligned with those outcomes along with credits and an indication of when they are offered. For more information on a course – including title, credits, description, and prerequisites – click its number. For more information on an outcome, click on its abbreviated name.

This sample pathway to a degree in CHHS is intended as a reference. It is unlikely that your actual pathway will look exactly like the model due to several factors, such as:

  • The number of credits you transfer into CSUMB, and how those credits articulate with CSUMB requirements
  • Whether or not you need to take additional courses to meet the CSUMB Language ULR as a Graduation Learning Outcome
  • Actual availability of courses during a given semester
  • Personal choices you make in your Individual Learning Plan – in consultation with your major advisors – including your choice of concentration within the major, and whether to take additional courses or pursue an academic minor in addition to a baccalaureate degree in CHHS.

All CSU students must earn nine upper-division General Education credits after having reached upper-division status. Upper-division GE courses taken when you are a lower-division student do not count toward this requirement, but students transferring as juniors should have no difficulty meeting this requirement, as several CHHS core courses qualify for GE credit.

This pathway assumes:

  • You have completed your General Education requirements, but must still complete the Language, Technology, Culture and Equity, and Service Learning ULRs as Graduation Learning Outcomes.
  • You have already completed an introductory Statistics course.
  • All program requirements can be satisfied during the academic year (fall/spring). (A limited number of program requirements can also be satisfied during the summer.)
