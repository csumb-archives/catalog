Earth Systems Science & Policy, B.S.~ Typical Pathways

Entering CSUMB as a Transfer

If you enter CSUMB as a junior transfer, please see the "Preparation" link at the left for coursework to complete before transferring.  Transfer students who wish to complete additional lower-division courses before transferring may take the courses marked by an asterisk (*) at a community college. 

The following generic pathway is applicable to all concentrations, which are listed under "Requirements" link at the left.  Students should consult an ESSP advisor for course scheduling details. 

Semester 1 (16 credits)

*GEOL 260+L Geology & Hydrology (MLO#2)
*Foreign Language 101 (Partial Language ULR)
*MATH 151+L Calculus II (MLO #1); not required for all concentrations
*ESSP 271 Oceans (MLO #2)
*ESSP 272 The Atmosphere (MLO#2)

Semester 2 (17 credits)

*Foreign Language 102 (Partial Language ULR)
ESSP 280 Economics, Policy, and Management (MLO #2)
ESSP 300 Reading, Writing, and Critical Thinking in ESSP (MLO#9 and GWAR)
ESSP 300L (Tech/Info ULR)
MLO 3 course

Semester 3 (16 credits)

*Foreign Language 201 (Completion of Language ULR)
*PHYS 220+L Physics I (MLO#2)
STAT 250  or 200, depending on concentration (MLO#1)
ESSP 332+L Intro to GIS/GPS (MLO#5)

Semester 4 (15 credits)

ESSP 384S Environmental Justice & Policy Service Learning (Culture& Equity  ENG COM B, Service Learning ULRs;  MLO#4&10)
MLO 8 course
MLO 7 course
ESSP 400 Capstone I (MLO#10)

Semester 5 (14 credits)

MLO 6 course
MLO#7 Group II course
MLO 7 Group II course
ESSP 403 Capstone II

* Course may be completed at community college (CAN GEOL 2, CAN MATH 20 or 32, CAN PHYS 2 or 8, SPAN 2, 4, 8; introductory oceanography, meteorology).

 
Entering CSUMB as a Freshman

The following sample lower-division pathway is applicable to all ESSP concentrations and emphases, except that some concentrations require only one semester of calculus.  The detailed requirements for each concentration are listed under the "Requirements" link at the left.  Students who work or participate in a lot of extracurricular activities will be advised to not take more than 14-16 units each semester. 

Semester 1 (16 credits)

Math 130+L Pre-calculus (Math Com ULR and MLO#1)
CST 101 Tech Tools (Tech/Info ULR)
FYS 100 First-Year Seminar
Foreign Language 101 (Partial Language ULR)

Semester 2 (17 credits)

Math 150+L Calculus I & Lab (MLO#1)
CHEM 110+L Chemistry I & Lab (MLO#2)
ESSP 286 Harvest Our Food: Fisheries/Agriculture (ENGCOM A and Ethics ULR)
Language 102 (Partial Language ULR)
HPWE Vibrancy Activity ULR

Semester 3 (14-18 credits)

Math 151+L Calculus II & Lab (MLO#1) (calc 2 not required for some concentrations)
CHEM 111+L Chemistry II & Lab (MLO#2)
Language 201 (Partial Language ULR)
ESSP 272 Atmosphere (MLO#2)
Creative/Artistic Expression and Literature/Popular Culture ULR course

Semester 4 (18 credits)

HPWE 361S Foundation of Wellness, Community Participation (Vibrancy Theory ULR)
BIO 240+L Biology & Lab (MLO#2)
ESSP 260 +L Geology/Hydrology (MLO #2)
Language 202 (Completion of Language ULR)
