Semester Credits Required

Minimum of 19 Credits.  At least 12 credits, including 6 upper-division credits, must be unique to the minor and not double-count for a student's major.  

Complete all of the following pre-requisite courses: 
  • MATH 130MATH 130: Precalculus
  • CHEM 110/LCHEM 110/L: Chemistry I
  • CHEM 111/LCHEM 111/L: Chemistry II

NOTE:  Pre-requisite courses do not count towards the credit requirement for the minor. 

Complete both of the following core biology courses, or their equivalents: 
  • BIO 210/LBIO 210/L: Molec&Cell Bio &Animal Phys
  • BIO 211/LBIO 211/L: Ecol, Evol, Biodiv & Plants
Complete at least three of the following for 9 to 12 credits* including at least one course with a lab component:
  • BIO 300BIO 300: Issues & Ethics in Biology
  • BIO 310BIO 310: Biochemistry
  • BIO 311BIO 311: Genetics
  • BIO 320BIO 320: Microbiology (lab)
  • BIO 330BIO 330: Comparative Physiology
  • BIO 340BIO 340: Ecology (lab)
  • BIO 342BIO 342: Plant Communities of CA (lab)
  • BIO 344LBIO 344L: Environmental Biotech (lab)
  • BIO 345BIO 345: Marine Biology (lab)
  • BIO 360BIO 360: Vertebrate Natural History (lab)
  • BIO 392BIO 392: Biology Seminar Series
  • BIO 395BIO 395: Special Topics
  • BIO 410BIO 410: Bioinformatics & Systems Bio (lab)
  • BIO 411BIO 411: Evolution Bio & Pop Genetics 
  • BIO 414BIO 414: Molecular Cell Biology
  • BIO 420BIO 420: Marine Invertebrate Zoology (lab)
  • BIO 430BIO 430: Marine Experimntl Physiology (lab)
  • BIO 448BIO 448: Aquatic Ecology (lab)
  • BIO 461BIO 461: Eukaryotic Molecular Biology (lab)
  • BIO 472BIO 472: California Flora (lab)
  • BIO 495BIO 495: Special Topics
  • BIO 494BIO 494: Biology Internship
  • Other BIO course numbered 300 or above with advisor approval

* The minor requires a minimum of 12 credits that do not double-count for the major. At least 6 of those 12 credits must be courses numbered 300 or above.  Most BIO minors who take BIO 210/LBIO 210/L: Molec&Cell Bio &Animal Phys and BIO 211/LBIO 211/L: Ecol, Evol, Biodiv & Plants for their major will need 12 upper-division credits that do not double-count for their major.
