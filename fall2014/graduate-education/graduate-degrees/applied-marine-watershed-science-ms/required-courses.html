In order to graduate, you will also need to complete your general education and university requirements.

Complete ALL of the following courses:
  • ENVS 500: Graduate Seminar 1
  • ENVS 502: Graduate Seminar II
  • ENVS 550: Research Methods
  • ENVS 580: Envir & Resource Eco:App&Meth
Complete ONE of the following course combinations:
  • ENVS 532: Advanced GIS&Spatial Analysis
  • ENVS 536: Remote Sns & Image Process 
  • MSCI 533: Seafloor Mapping 

Students  select one of the following areas of emphasis and complete two courses (which may not double-count) 

Watershed Emphasis
  • ENVS 560: Watershed Systems
  • ENVS 660: Adv Watershed Sci & Policy
Marine Emphasis
  • MSCI 545: Marine Ecological Systems 
  • MSCI 645: Adv Marine Sci & Pol
Students that select the thesis option must complete 6 credits of ENVS 599: Masters Thesis and ONE of the following courses not counted towards their emphasis:
  • PHYS 530: Electronics for Grad Research
  • BIO 548: Aquatic Ecology 
  • ENVS 532: Advanced GIS&Spatial Analysis 
  • MSCI 533: Seafloor Mapping 
  • ENVS 536: Remote Sns & Image Process
  • ENVS 540: Environmental Modeling
  • ENVS 542: Vernal Pools of Fort Ord
  • ENVS 560: Watershed Systems
  • ENVS 561: Watershed Systems Restoration 
  • MSCI 545: Marine Ecological Systems 
  • ENVS 586: Coastal Zone Policy & Mangmt
  • OR an MLML, MIIS, or MPP course approved by advisor 
Students that select the PSM option must complete 2 credits of ENVS 594: Internship - Graduate and TWO of the following not counting for their emphasis:
  • PHYS 530: Electronics for Grad Research
  • BIO 548: Aquatic Ecology
  • ENVS 532: Advanced GIS&Spatial Analysis
  • MSCI 533: Seafloor Mapping
  • ENVS 536: Remote Sns & Image Process
  • ENVS 540: Environmental Modeling
  • ENVS 542: Vernal Pools of Fort Ord
  • ENVS 560: Watershed Systems
  • MSCI 545: Marine Ecological Systems
  • ENVS 586: Coastal Zone Policy & Mangmt
  • OR an MLML, MIIS, or MPP course approved by advisor
