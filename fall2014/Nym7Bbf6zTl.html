If you transferred into CSUMB as an AA-T-certified student in History, you must complete the following courses for your bachelor's degree in Human Communication at CSUMB. If you are unsure about your transfer status, please talk to an advisor. 

Complete ALL of the following courses:
  • HCOM 300HCOM 300: Major ProSeminar
  • HCOM 312HCOM 312: Cooperative Argumentation
  • HCOM 350SHCOM 350S: Oral Hist/Comm Mem SL *
  • HCOM 475HCOM 475: Senior Capstone
  • SBS 350SBS 350: Domination & Resistance: US (Fulfills American Institutions, GE Area D 2)
Complete ONE of the following courses:
  • HCOM 306HCOM 306: Gender & Communication
  • HCOM 309HCOM 309: Interracial Communication
  • HCOM 310HCOM 310: Free Speech & Resp
Complete ONE of the following courses:
  • HCOM 301HCOM 301: Ways Of Knowing
  • HCOM 406HCOM 406: Philosophy Acrdng to Movies
Complete ONE of the following courses:
  • HCOM 338HCOM 338: Multicultural Adolescent Lit
  • HCOM 342HCOM 342: Feminist Theories & Methods
  • HCOM 343HCOM 343: Race & Gender Justice
  • HCOM 344HCOM 344: Chicana/Latina Experiences
  • HCOM 345HCOM 345: Chicano Life & Culture
  • HCOM 348HCOM 348: Race, Colonialism & Film
Complete ONE of the following courses:
  • HCOM 322HCOM 322: Asian American Literature
  • HCOM 323HCOM 323: American Drama
  • HCOM 324HCOM 324: African American Narratives
  • HCOM 325HCOM 325: 20th Cent Narr Amer Immgr *
  • HCOM 326HCOM 326: Jewish Holocaust Literature
  • HCOM 327HCOM 327: Survey American Literature *
  • HCOM 335HCOM 335: Amer Ethnic Lit & Culture
  • HCOM 336HCOM 336: Poetry & Gender
  • HCOM 337HCOM 337: Women's Literature
Complete ONE of the following courses:
  • HCOM 330HCOM 330: Intro Creative Writing
  • only with prior consent of the instructor: HCOM 332HCOM 332: Poetry Writing Workshop, HCOM 333HCOM 333: Women's Writing Workshop, HCOM 334HCOM 334: Fiction/Creative NonFiction Wr or HCOM 432HCOM 432: Soc Action Wrtg

Concentrations

History, Oral History, and New Media Concentration
Complete ONE course from each of the following groups (one of which must be designated as Research/Theory Intensive with an *):

Group A. History

  • HCOM 346HCOM 346: African American Life/History
  • HCOM 352HCOM 352: History According To Movies
  • HCOM 353HCOM 353: California At Crossroads
  • HCOM 359HCOM 359: Sexuality, Law and History
  • HCOM 363HCOM 363: Topics in Social History*
  • HCOM 365HCOM 365: Chicana/o Latina/o History*
  • HCOM 366HCOM 366: History of Religion in US
  • HCOM 367HCOM 367: Gender and U.S. History
  • HCOM 445HCOM 445: Slavery & Race in the Americas *
  • HCOM 451HCOM 451: Transnational Migrations *

Group B. Historical Narrative

  • HCOM 324HCOM 324: African American Narratives
  • HCOM 325HCOM 325: 20th Cent Narr Amer Immgr
  • HCOM 336HCOM 336: Poetry & Gender
  • HCOM 344HCOM 344: Chicana/Latina Experiences *
  • HCOM 348HCOM 348: Race, Colonialism & Film:*
  • HCOM 426HCOM 426: Travel Narratives
  • HCOM 432HCOM 432: Soc Action Wrtg
  • HCOM 432SHCOM 432S: Social Action Writing *

Group C. Multimedia

  • HCOM 328HCOM 328: Latina Life Stories
  • HCOM 329HCOM 329: Auto/Biografias
  • HCOM 356HCOM 356: Multicultural History

 

Students must also demonstrate second language proficiency through the 201-level

Read more about the Language Proficiency Requirement through the 201 level. If you need to begin language study at the 101 level, consult your department chair to determine your 60 unit pathway.
