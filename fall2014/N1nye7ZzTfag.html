In order to graduate, you will also need to complete your general education and university requirements.

Major Requirements are identified below. In order to graduate, students must also meet all general education and university requirements. 

Complete ALL of the following courses:
  • WLC 300: WLC Major ProSeminar
  • JAPN 320S: Advanced Japanese SL
  • WLC 400: WLC Major Capstone
Complete TWO of the following courses
  • JAPN 300: Intro to Adv Communication
  • JAPN 302: History of Japan
  • JAPN 380: Japan: Land and People
  • JAPN 402: Japanese Literature
Complete TWO of the following courses:
  • JAPN 301: Adv Japanese Lang Cult & Com
  • JAPN 303: Business Japanese
  • JAPN 312: Japanese for the Professions
  • JAPN 304: Intro Translate & Interpret
  • JAPN 401: Structure of Japanese Lang
Complete FOUR of the following courses (only one 200-level course):
  • JAPN 205: Intro Japanese Culture & Civ
  • JAPN 305: Intro Japanese Clture/Civ
  • JAPN 212: Samurai Spirit
  • JAPN 215: Cool Japan Today
  • JAPN 306: The Japanese Mind
  • JAPN 307: Japan-American Experierence
  • JAPN 308: Japanese Pop-Culture
  • JAPN 309: Japanese Lit in Translation
  • JAPN 310: Japanese Cinema
  • JAPN 311: Social Issues In Japan
  • JAPN 213: Manga, Anime & Modern Japan
  • JAPN 313: Manga, Anime & Modern Japan
  • JAPN 314: JPN Visual Culture & Media
  • JAPN 405: Economic History Of Japan
  • JAPN 407: Japan in Asia
  • JAPN 408: Business In Japan
Complete ONE of the following courses:
  • SPAN 307: History&Politics Of Mex
  • SPAN 308: Hist/Cultr Aztlan:SW US
  • SPAN 309: Hist & Polit Of Latn Amer
  • SPAN 311: US Foreign Policy Pac Rim
  • SPAN 365: The Chicano Novel
  • SPAN 370: The Chicano Community
  • HCOM 322: Asian American Literature
  • And other Upper Division non-JAPN courses
Compete ONE of the following courses:
  • JAPN 400: Adv Interpersonal Com
  • JAPN 403: Pre-Capstone
  • JAPN 404: Advanced Japanese Research

Though only one course is required, students are encouraged to take two courses (one in Fall and one in Spring) in the same year they are working on their Capstone projects.

Students demonstrate that they have actively immersed themselves in authentic Japanese cultural and linguistic environments and have internalized the language and cultural experience, from which they have developed a personal understanding and new perspectives of the community.

Students are strongly encouraged to enroll in JAPN 420: Maximizing Study Abroad prior to their Study Abroad experience.
