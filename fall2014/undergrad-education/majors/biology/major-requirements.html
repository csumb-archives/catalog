In order to graduate, you will also need to complete your general education and university requirements.

The Biology major begins in the freshman year with specific math and science requirements because all upper-division (junior/senior) biology courses require lower-division (freshman/sophomore) courses as prerequisites.  

Biology students complete the 'core' courses listed below and select a CONCENTRATION

Complete ALL of the following courses:
  • CHEM 110/L: Chemistry I
  • CHEM 111/L: Chemistry II
  • BIO 210/L: Molec&Cell Bio &Animal Phys
  • BIO 211/L: Ecol, Evol, Biodiv & Plants
  • BIO 311: Genetics
Complete ONE of the following, which also fulfils the CSU Graduation Writing Assessment Requirement (GWAR):
  • BIO 300: Issues & Ethics in Biology
  • ENVS 300: Crit Thkg & Comm in Env Sci
  • MSCI 300: Marine Sci, Comm, & Policy
Complete ONE of the following Service Learning courses:
  • BIO 378S: Health Sciences SL
  • BIO 379S: Biology Service Learning
  • ENSTU 349S: Environmental Interpretation
  • ENSTU 384S: Social and Ecol Justice
  • ENSTU 369S: Com-Based Wshed Restor SL
  • MATH 361S: Mathematics Tutors
  • MSCI 359S: Marine Service Learning
Complete ONE of the following Capstone seminar courses:
  • BIO 491: Biology Research Capstone
  • BIO 492: Course-Based Capstone
  • BIO 493: Biology Test-based Capstone
  • ENVS 410: Capstone Integration Seminar
  • ENSTU 403: Capstone Seminar 2

 

SELECT A CONCENTRATION  and complete the specified coursework.  The concentrations offered are: Molecular Biology; Ecology, Evolution, and Organismal Biology; and Teacher Preparation.

MOLECULAR BIOLOGY CONCENTRATION:
Complete the following courses: 
  • MATH 150: Calculus I
  • STAT 100: Introduction to Statistics OR  STAT 250: Applied Stat:Sci Tech
  • PHYS 150: Physics for Biologists OR BOTH  PHYS 220: Physics I AND PHYS 221: Physics II
  • BIO 310: Biochemistry OR  BIO 414: Molecular Cell Biology
  • BIO 330: Comparative Physiology OR  BIO 411: Evolution Bio & Pop Genetics
Complete ONE of the following course combinations:
  • CHEM 210/L: Organic Chem for Biologists AND BIO 310: Biochemistry
  • CHEM 211: Organic Chemistry I AND CHEM 312: Organic Chemistry II
Complete ONE of the following courses:
  • BIO 320: Microbiology
  • BIO 344L: Environmental Biotech
  • BIO 461: Eukaryotic Molecular Biology
  • BIO 410: Bioinformatics & Systems Bio
  • BIO 430: Marine Experimntl Physiology
  • MSCI 430: Marine Experimental Physiol
