In order to graduate, you will also need to complete your general education and university requirements.

Complete ALL of the following lower and upper division courses and course combinations:

Lower-division core (must be completed before moving on to upper-division core or area courses):

  • PSY 100: Introduction to Psychology
  • PSY 200: Intro/Research/Methd/Data/Anal AND PSY 200L: Research Method Lab
  • PSY 300: Expermntl/Psy & Human/Assesmnt
  • PSY 301: Adv/Psychological Statistics AND PSY 301L: Adv/Psychological Stat Lab OR PSY 305: PSY Testing & Measurement AND PSY 305L: PSY Testing & Measurement Lab
  • PSY 302: PSY/Resear/Methods/Data/Analys AND PSY 302L: Psychology Research Lab
  • PSY 400: Psychology Capstone

 

 Complete ONE of the following courses from EACH of the following FIVE AREAS:

Biological Psychology

  • PSY 310: Biological Psychology
  • PSY 315: Psychopharmacology

Clinical Psychology

  • PSY 320: Psychopathology
  • PSY 321: Clinical Psychology
  • PSY 322: Theories/Methds of Counselng
  • PSY 329: Health Psychology

Cognitive Psychology

  • PSY 330: Cognitive Psychology
  • PSY 331: Human Memory
  • PSY 332: Decsn Making & Prblm Solving
  • PSY 333: Cognitive Neuroscience
  • PSY 334: Sensation & Perception

Developmental Psychology

  • PSY 340: Developmental Psychology
  • PSY 341: Psych of Thought & Language
  • PSY 346: Cognitive Development
  • PSY 347: Social & Emotional Development

Social Psychology

  • PSY 350: Social Psychology
  • PSY 352: Theories of Personality
  • PSY 353: Soc Psy/ Gender
  • PSY 354: SocPsy/CrimJustice
  • PSY 355: Social Psy and Soc Justice
