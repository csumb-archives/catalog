Credits Required

Total credits in the minor: 16

Prerequisites

None

Outcomes, Courses and Assessment

The two outcomes listed below encompass the knowledge, skills and attitudes that students are expected to master to successfully complete the minor in Global Studies. Students must demonstrate competency in the following:

LO 1 Core understanding of global processes ~ Demonstrate the ability to comprehend and apply key concepts, skills and knowledge in global processes in global history, culture, political economy or politics.  

  • Complete two of following: 
     GS 316: Historical Approaches
    GS 330: World Views
    GS 370: Global Political Economy
    GS 390: Global Politics

LO 2 Focused knowledge of global processes ~ Demonstrate focused knowledge and and key concepts in global processes.  Choose any two GS courses listed.

  • Complete any two of following:
    GS 311: US/Foreig/Poli/Pac/Rim/3rd/Wrl
    GS 320: Global Issues & 3rd World
    GS 322: Dilemmas in Humanitarianism
    GS 325: International Development
    GS 326: US Foreign Policy & Empire
    GS 332: Religion/Post Secular World
    GS 335: Comparative/Gov'ts & Politics
    GS 340: Social Medial and Global Comm
    GS 343: Global Film & Film Indust
    GS 345: Glob Politcal Theory/Philosphy
    GS 349: The West/Arab/Muslim World
    GS 350: Gender/Violence in Global Life
    GS 360: Religion/Violence/Peacemaking
    GS 362: Global Literatures
    GS 375: Ecological Political Economy
    GS 376: Political Economy of the US
    GS 380S: Int'l Organizations & SL
    GS 385: Global Ecology
    GS 395: Special Topics

Assessment ~ The Assessment Plan will evaluate time to completion, reason for choice and content.
