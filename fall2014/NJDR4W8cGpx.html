Credits Required

Total credits in the minor: 16

Prerequisites

None

Outcomes, Courses and Assessment

The two outcomes listed below encompass the knowledge, skills and attitudes that students are expected to master to successfully complete the minor in Global Studies. Students must demonstrate competency in the following:

LO 1 Core understanding of global processes ~ Demonstrate the ability to comprehend and apply key concepts, skills and knowledge in global processes in global history, culture, political economy or politics.  

  • Complete two of following: 
     GS 316GS 316: Historical Approaches
    GS 330GS 330: World Views
    GS 370GS 370: Global Political Economy
    GS 390GS 390: Global Politics

LO 2 Focused knowledge of global processes ~ Demonstrate focused knowledge and and key concepts in global processes.  Choose any two GS courses listed.

  • Complete any two of following:
    GS 311GS 311: US/Foreig/Poli/Pac/Rim/3rd/Wrl
    GS 320GS 320: Global Issues & 3rd World
    GS 322GS 322: Dilemmas in Humanitarianism
    GS 325GS 325: International Development
    GS 326GS 326: US Foreign Policy & Empire
    GS 332GS 332: Religion/Post Secular World
    GS 335GS 335: Comparative/Gov'ts & Politics
    GS 340GS 340: Social Medial and Global Comm
    GS 343GS 343: Global Film & Film Indust
    GS 345GS 345: Glob Politcal Theory/Philosphy
    GS 349GS 349: The West/Arab/Muslim World
    GS 350GS 350: Gender/Violence in Global Life
    GS 360GS 360: Religion/Violence/Peacemaking
    GS 362GS 362: Global Literatures
    GS 375GS 375: Ecological Political Economy
    GS 376GS 376: Political Economy of the US
    GS 380SGS 380S: Int'l Organizations & SL
    GS 385GS 385: Global Ecology
    GS 395GS 395: Special Topics

Assessment ~ The Assessment Plan will evaluate time to completion, reason for choice and content.
