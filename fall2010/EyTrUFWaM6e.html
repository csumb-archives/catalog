The Health and Wellness minor offers courses that focus on both personal wellbeing and the skills to implement the content into chosen majors regardless of career or life goals. Anyone wanting to live optimally and assist family, community and personal wellbeing will benefit.

Once this minor has been declared, a minimum of 12 credits covering both theoretical and practical applications are required. These can come from the following content offerings:

  • Models and Dimensions of Wellness
  • Consumer Advocacy and Wellbeing
  • Effectively Managing Stress
  • Human Sexuality
  • Patterns and Dynamics of Relationships
  • Behavior Change and Support Group Strategies
  • Multicultural and Ancestral Views of Well-Being & Healing
  • Holistic Health and Wellness
  • Health & Wellness Internship

You will find all the official information about this minor by using the left-side navigation on this page.

For additional information including a look at faculty bios, please visit the Kinesiology Department website.

"While the lives of college students are challenged and stressed by a wide variety of demands and expectations, a minor in health and wellness seeks to assist students in balancing them by providing relevant knowledge and skills. It is anticipated that this enrichment in their curriculum will both improve the quality of their personal lives as well as contribute to the wellbeing of those with whom they work."
- Barbara Sayad, Ph.D., M.P.H., Health & Wellness Faculty
