The Social Work minor provides an interdisciplinary introduction to core knowledge and skills you will need to begin a professional career in social work, counseling, case management, client advocacy or corrections. The curriculum emphasizes entry-level practice skills, professional ethics and values, human behavior and development, and an internship experience.

Integration with Majors

The Social Work minor complements and builds upon the strengths of Human Development, Human Communication, Social and Behavioral Sciences and other major degree programs, equipping you with a unique and practical skill set. SW also provides a solid background for Business Administration majors interested in the nonprofit sector.

Sponsored By

College of Professional Studies > Health, Human Services & Public Policy Department

Online Components

Some courses are offered in whole or in part online.
