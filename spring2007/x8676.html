WORLD THEATER STAFF BIOS

A native of New Haven, Connecticut, DAWN GIBSON-BREHON (director) is a graduate of the Hartt School of Music and University of Wisconsin/Madison Center for Arts Administration, majoring in music management and arts administration respectively.  Prior to her arrival at CSU Monterey, Mrs. Gibson-Brehon managed a 35-event performing arts series at Palm Beach Community College’s Duncan Theatre in Lake Worth, Florida.  Mrs. Gibson-Brehon’s past work experience includes programming and artist services at George Mason University’s Institute of the Arts in Fairfax, Virginia; site visitor for the Rockefeller and Pew Charitable Trust Foundations; marketing and public relations for the Central City Opera in Denver, Colorado and Center Stage in Baltimore, Maryland; booking manager for the Lar Lubovitch Dance Company in New York City; general management for the Pascal Rioult Dance Theatre in New York City; guest faculty member for the Arts Presenters’ Basic Course; and operations management for the University of Wisconsin-Madison School of Music Opera.  She has served as a grant panelist for the Association of Performing Arts Presenters, Dance Advance/Pew Charitable Trust, the National Endowment for the Arts, the State of Florida Division of Cultural Affairs and the State of Ohio Arts Council.  She has also participated in workshop panels at local, regional and national conferences.  Mrs. Gibson-Brehon is a former board member of the Association of Performing Arts Presenters and Chamber Music America, as well as a member of Leadership Palm Beach County/Class of 1999, Arts Leadership for the Future/Class 2002, California Presenters, the Marina Business Association and Strathmore’s Who’s Who Directory.  In March 2000 she was recognized by JM Family Enterprises and awarded the African-American Achiever Award in the Arts category for making a difference in the community and improving the quality of life for South Florida residents.  In 2002 she received the Marina Business Association’s Businesswoman of the Year Award.  In her spare time, she likes to read mystery novels and spend quality time with her three children.

PHIL ESPARZA (media production specialist and operations manager) has been in the arts for over 33 years.  He began his career as a technician, actor and company member of El Teatro Campesino.  He has been associated with Luis Valdez and El Teatro Campesino for all his adult life and has developed his skills as a producer, managing director and now a board member.  He has been fortunate to have been heavily involved with such groundbreaking projects as: El Teatro on tour around the world (over 100 different live, video and film productions), Zoot Suit (play & movie), La Bamba (the film) and The Cisco Kid (TNT Film).  He has been extremely, pleased to work at the Japan American Theater, Marines Memorial Theater, The Mark Taper Forum, The Winter Garden Theater and Old Globe Theater in various audience development roles and as a producer.  Besides being a board member for El Teatro, has been on the boards of WAA, CALAA, California Theater Council and the Pacific Peoples Theater Festival.  He has served on various panels on the local, state, national and international theater and film scenes.

CHAS CROSLIN (box office manager and budget analyst) received his BA in the History of Art from the University of Michigan and worked for four years at the University's Museum of Art, serving as a student assistant, Assistant to the Senior Curator of Asian Art, and Project Assistant for the museum’s digital imaging project. His past work experience includes Money Room Manager for Saginaw Harness Raceway, in Saginaw, Michigan, two years as Theater Manager in various cinemas for Harkins Amusement Enterprises in Phoenix, Arizona, and two and a half years as Theater Manager for the Northwest Film Center of the Portland Art Museum in Portland, Oregon. While at the Film Center, Chas worked with a team to coordinate and organize operations for three consecutive years of the Portland International Film Festival. At CSUMB, Chas helped to curate the 3rd Annual Monterey Bay Film Festival in the fall of 2002. He has produced and performed in various theater and entertainment events on campus and assists in teaching a repeating undergraduate course in the art of producing and presenting arts events. Chas has recently begun coursework towards obtaining a Master of Arts degree in Humanities.
