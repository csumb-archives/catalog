Professional Development Opportunities

CSUMB is committed to the personal and professional development of our staff and faculty. As an institution of higher education we support and promote lifelong learning for our employees. Free on-site seminars, certification programs, workshops, and financial support for off-campus training are available.  Register for a course

New Course Registration Process

Starting at the end of September 2006, the Professional Development Program will begin using PeopleSoft Self-Service to list courses, manage enrollment, and track training histories for all participants.  The current FileMaker Pro database will be retired and all records incorporated in PeopleSoft HR.

To ease this transition, we have created a Professional Development calendar as part of our website with links to PeopleSoft Self-Service.  The calendar pages on the left tool bar will show the upcoming classes, dates, and locations. 

When you select a class, you will be directed to the log-in page for PeopleSoft Self-Service.  After signing in, choose MB Self Service > Personal Portfolio> Home>Personal Portfolio>Training Summary> You can then search for course sessions and enroll

CSU Fee Waiver Program

This program allows CSU employees to enroll in university coursework for which fees are waived or reduced. The following employees are eligible to participate subject to management approval: full-time or part-time permanent staff employees, full-time probationary employees, full-time managers, tenured and probationary faculty, and temporary faculty with at least six years of full-time equivalent service.

Eligible faculty employees may also transfer their fee waiver benefit entitlement to a spouse or dependent child. Employee eligibility is contingent upon employee category. Employees can generally request a waiver of application fees and tuition for up to two courses or six units (whichever is greater) per semester. Courses must be job-related and/or part of an individual career development plan. Winter session and extended education courses are excluded. The Fee Waiver Program is a negotiated benefit and is subject to change.
