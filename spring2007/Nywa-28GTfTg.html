WORLD THEATER ANNOUNCES 2006/2007 PERFORMING ARTS SERIES


   Seaside, California, June 5, 2006 -- California State University, Monterey Bay is pleased to announce its sixth annual Performing Arts Series.  The 2006/2007 Season’s theme Welcome to the World! will spotlight certain cultures and voices from around the globe.  Ticket costs range from $25 to $15 and can be purchased by calling the World Theater box office at (831) 582-4580 or online at www.csumb.edu/worldtheater.

Since its inception in spring 2002, the World Theater has established itself as a world-class performing arts center that supports diverse presentations and educational activities.  States director Dawn Gibson-Brehon, “We have an exciting line up artists for this coming year.  We have always supported diverse cultures and experiences through our programming and outreach programs.  Our goal this year is to deepen our commitment by providing more opportunities for our audiences to learn about differences and to interact with professional performing artists.”  The season will include international artists Son de Madera, a Mexican music ensemble from Veracruz and Compagnie TchéTché, a contemporary all female dance company from Africa’s Ivory Coast.  “The season will also include a special silent film screening with live orchestra at the historic Golden State Theatre in Monterey.  The Paragon Ragtime Orchestra is regarded as one America’s premiere groups,” state Gibson-Brehon.  “We will screen silent films from the masters of the day – Buster Keaton, Charlie Chaplin and Harold Lloyd, while the Paragon Ragtime Orchestra will accompany the films with the original music scores.”  The Series will also include the return of the Grammy-nominated Imani Winds in a new work celebrating the life and legacy of performer Josephine Baker; Andes Manta, an evening of music rooted in the cultural heritage of the Incas; and a one person theater show about American poet and writer Anne Sexton, among others.

The World Theater 2006/2007 Performing Arts Series and related outreach activities are supported in part by KAZU Radio, 90.3 FM, Radio Bilingue of Salinas, the Cannery Row Company, the National Dance Project of the New England Foundation for the Arts, Integrity Printing, the Seaside Post, the Marina Gazette and the Pebble Beach Company Foundation.

The World Theater is located on the campus of CSU Monterey Bay in Seaside, California.  Tickets for the performances range from $25 to $15.  For more information or to purchase tickets, please call the World Theater box office at (831) 582-4580.
