WORLD THEATER
2006/2007 ARTS FOR KIDS SERIES

Supported in part by:

       

 


Imani Winds
Friday, October 27, 2006
10:30am (open to grades 1 - 2)


This Grammy-nominated five-member wind ensemble performs classical and contemporary music by Latino and African-American composers.  Instrumentation includes clarinet, oboe, French horn, flute and bassoon. The program also includes short classical and world music pieces for woodwind quintet.

 

Nai-Ni Chen Dance Company
Thursday, March 8, 2007
10:30am (open to grades 1 - 2)


A performance that successfully combines the dynamic freedom of American modern dance with the grace and splendor of Asian Art, bridging the gap of understanding between East and West.  School programs foster an appreciation for Chinese culture and history.

 

Andes Manta
Friday, May 4, 2007
10:30am (open to grades 3 - 5)

The vibrant, powerful music of the Andes is played from Colombia to Tierra del Fuego, and none play it better than the four Lopez brothers who make up Andes Manta. The school concert will include explanations and demonstrations about each native instrument.  The musicians also discuss life in their home country of Ecuador.


Background
The Series, an expansion of a four-year partnership with Crumpton Elementary School, includes live evens with professionial touring artists at the World Theater.   Attendance for all activities is free and and bus transportation for participant schools is provided by the World Theater.  In addition, the World Theater will provide study guides that will be distributed free of charge to each participant school prior to presentation.

Reservations for the Arts For Kids Series will be accepted on a first come, first serve basi and notices will be faxed to all elementary schools in Monterey County in August 2006.  If you have any questions regarding the upcoming events or would like to be included on the fax list, please contact Dawn Gibson-Brehon at dawn_gibson-brehon@csumb.edu.

With generous support from the Pebble Beach Company Foundation, Integrity Printing, the UPJOHN California Fund and World Theater Donors, the World Theater is pleased to offer its Arts For Kids Series for a second consecutive year.  A special thanks to our funders for supporting arts and education programs for area school children.
