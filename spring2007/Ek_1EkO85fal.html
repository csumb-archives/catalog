Prospective Employees

CSUMB

Founded in 1994, California State University, Monterey Bay (CSUMB) became the 21st University in the CSU system. CSUMB is committed to serving the diverse people of California, especially the working class, historically under-served, and low-income populations. (For interesting facts regarding the 23 campuses of the California State University (CSU), please refer to Campuses of the CSU.)

Location

The Monterey Peninsula is one of the most diverse and beautiful areas in California, noted for its dramatic coastlines, scenic mountain views, and consistent climate (55°-75°F). CSUMB is located between Seaside and Marina, just north of the Monterey Peninsula. The Seaside and Marina beaches offer long stretches of flat sand and beautiful dunes.

Benefits

CSUMB offers a comprehensive and competitive benefits package which includes health, dental, vision, Flex Cash, dependent care and retirement plans for certain categories of employees. Eligibility is subject to collective bargaining unit and policy. Employees may also be eligible for other benefit programs, such as employer-paid life and long-term disability insurance, depending on their employee category. For more detailed information, please refer to the Benefits web site.

Housing

CSUMB offers its faculty and staff two housing options on campus: rental units and homes for purchase via a ground-lease plan. The campus neighborhoods provide employees with workplace-convenient, continuously affordable housing. The program also facilitates employee recruitment, cultivates interaction among employees and the larger university community, and enables greater employee participation in campus affairs. For additional information, please refer to the CEHI website.
