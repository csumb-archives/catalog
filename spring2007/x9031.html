Benefits & Wellness Fair, 9/20/06, 11am - 2pm, University Center

University Human Resources invites all Staff, Faculty, and their families to CSUMB's Benefits & Wellness Fair, Wednesday, September 20th from 11am - 2pm in the University Center.  The purpose of this fair is to showcase  CSUMB's benefit providers as well as the different programs and companies that promote healthy lifestyles in the Monterey County area, this year totaling 45+ vendor participants!

This year, we will be having an Asian New Year's festival, celebrating "The Year of Longevity." Our goal is to encourage employees and their families to make, renew, and/or maintain healthy lifestyle habits. To assist in accomplishing this goal, we have FREE massages, seminars, food, and raffle prizes!  We'll even have an origami paper crane-making station where employees can make a paper crane, symbolizing the new and/or existing healthy habits in their lives.

New this year, we will have a blood drive courtesy of the CHOMP Blood Center and the First Annual Workplace Walk-off Challenge, where teams of five compete to collectively walk the most steps, earning the title of "Workplace Walk-off Challenge Champions."

For the latest information on vendors, prizes, seminars, and more,  please visit the Benefits & Wellness Fair website at: csumb.edu/benefitsfair.

2006 Open Enrollment Period September 1st - October 6th, 2006

It's that time again!  The annual Open Enrollment period for 2006 will begin September 1st and will run through October 6th with an effective date of January 1st, 2007.  During Open Enrollment you may:

  • Enroll, if benefit eligible and not currently enrolled
  • Change plans
  • Add or delete eligible family members **(additional documentation may be required- see below)
  • Cancel CSU coverage
  • Enroll or re-enroll in the Flexible Spending Accounts (Health Care/ Dependent Care Reimbursement Accounts - HCRA/ DCRA)

Should you wish to participate in Open Enrollment for any of the above reasons, you will need to fill out the appropriate form(s) found on the UHR website under "Forms" which are noted below: 

  • Health enrollments/changes/ cancellations - 2006 Open Enrollment Worksheet
  • Dental enrollments/ changes/ cancellations - Dental Enrollment Authorization Form
  • Flexcash enrollments/changes/ cancellations - Flexcash Enrollment Authorization Form
  • HCRA/ DCRA enrollments/ changes/ cancellations - Health Care/ Dependent Care Enrollment Authorization Form

All Open Enrollment forms must be completed, signed and received in the UHR Benefits office in bld. 23 no later than Friday, October 6th, 2006.  Incomplete forms or forms received after the deadline will not be accepted.

 **Enrolling Domestic Partner Family Members- Eligible employees may add domestic partners and children of domestic partners to their health & dental plan using the same-sex enrollment policies currently used for traditional marriages.  Domestic partnerships include specified same-sex domestic partners between persons who are both at least age 18 and not legally married, and specified opposite sex domestic partners when both individuals are age 62 or older.  If you are adding a domestic partner, the partnership must be registered with the Secretary of State.  A copy of the filed registration must accompany your enrollment documents before health, dental and/or vision coverage can become effective.  Detailed information about legal requirements and how to register can be found at http://www.ss.ca.gov/dpregistry/.

In addition, Domestic Partners will need to fill out and submit with their enrollment request an Affidavit of Liability (for health only) and a Domestic Partner Dependent Tax Certification form as certain tax liabilities may be incurred.  For specific questions, please contact your tax advisor.

 

**Enrolling a Spouse- If you are adding a spouse, a copy of your marriage certificate must accompany your enrollment documents before health, dental and/or vision coverage can become effective.

 

**Enrolling an Economically Dependent Child - If you are adding an economically dependent child, please fill out and submit the Affidavit of Eligibility for Economically Dependent Children which must accompany your enrollment(s).

 

Should you have any questions, please contact Lindsay Scarr at x3526 or Terry Burns at x3387.

New HCRA/ DCRA Toll-Free Fax Number for Claims Submission

Effective July 1, 2006, a toll-free fax number will be available to HCRA/ DCRA participants for claim submission.  Faxing your claim submission saves money, time and trees.  It also allows you to keep your original for your tax records.  The new toll-free fax number is 1-866-381-9682.  Please keep this for your records.

Direct Deposit Option for HCRA/ DCRA Participants

Want to receive your claim reimbursement even faster?  HCRA/ DCRA participants have the option to sign up for direct deposit, which could get your money in your bank account the day after you fax it!  (ASI guarantees processing of claims the business day following receipt.  Deposits are effective the banking day following processing.)  The form can be found on the ASI website at: http://asiflex.com/forms/directdepositemail.doc.  Please mail or fax it to the address or number on the form.

CalPERS Long Term Care 2006 Open Enrollment Dates

The CalPERS Long Term Care open enrollment begins July 1 through September 30 2006. All California public employees, retirees, their spouses, parents, parents-in-law, and adult siblings (age 18 - 79) are eligible for this CalPERS Program. You can request to have information materials mailed to you , including an information guide, plan options, an application, and more by calling the Kit Request phone line at (800) 266-1050. For more information go to: http://www.beginswithaplan.com/.

New CalPERS Employer Contribution Rates

Effective with the first pay period that ends on July 2006, the CSU will contribute 16.997% towards the retirement benefits of State Miscellaneous Member First Tier members. CSU employees, except those who belong to unit 8, who are eligible for CalPERS membership are enrolled as first tier members. The 2005-2006 contribution rate was 15.942%. The employer contribution for state safety members (includes police officers) will change to 19.294%.

ALERT: Delta Dental Automated Response System Down

On November 14, 2005, Delta Dental reprogrammed their system so that any inquiry coming in through the automated phone line for a CSU member would automatically be forwarded to a Customer Service Representative. The purpose of this was to allow CSU members (and their providers) direct contact with Customer Service, as the automated system was not allowing members and providers to get beyond the birth date field.

According to Delta, changes to the automated voice response system that were previously implemented in November are not functioning properly at this time.  Unfortunately, due to a recent change made to the automated system, CSU members are not automatically forwarded to a Delta Customer Service Representative.  Consequently, members receive incorrect eligibility information. In response to this issue, Delta has submitted an emergency fix request to their systems vendor and hopefully this will be resolved by next week.  

We apologize for any inconvenience that you may encounter in using your dental benefits. Should you or your provider receive eligibility information that you feel is incorrect, please call Kristi McMahon, Benefits Assistant, so that she can intercede to resolve the problem. It is best to confirm eligibility and call the Benefits Office for assistance before your dental appointment particularly if this is the first time you or your dependents are using your dental benefit.
