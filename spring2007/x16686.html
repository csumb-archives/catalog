THE SIGHTS AND SOUNDS OF EASTERN MEXICO
TO GRACE WORLD THEATER STAGE

   Seaside, California, August 18, 2006 -- California State University, Monterey Bay is pleased to present Son de Madera on Thursday, September 21, 2006 at 7:30pm.  Utilizing the rare and beautiful instruments of Eastern Mexico, Son de Madera represents the leading success in the current "Son Jarocho" movement (a musical and dance genre that blends elements of Spanish melodies and African rhythms).  This talented group from Veracruz, Mexico will transport viewers to a magical place with their unique and sensuous sound, making their concerts appealing to listeners of all ages.  Tickets range from $25 - $10 and can be purchased by calling the World Theater box office at (831) 582-4580 or online at CSUMB.edu/worldtheater.

Founded in 1992 in Veracruz, Mexico, Son de Madera’s main objective is the diffusion of the "Son Jarocho" based on the research of the traditional music and the incorporation of new sounds that enrich this music.  The repertoire of Son de Madera comes mainly from the songs of the traditional fandango (musical fiesta around the dance’s platform) on the south of Veracruz.  The group has appeared in the United States at the Chicago Old Town School of Music, Los Cenzontles Mexican Arts Center, La Peña Cultural Center in Berkeley and at the Mexican Heritage Plaza in San Jose, California.  In addition to the group’s work in the touring and recording scenes, Son de Madera has participated in joint projects with other musicians, such as Irish music group the Cassidies and Quetzal.  Son de Madera’s performances are sensuous and with an exquisite sense of musicianship and have been featured on numerous television and radio programs in Mexico and the United States.  Their recordings have been used in films soundtracks, television documentaries and theater.  Support for the Son de Madera concert made is possible in part by Radio Bilingue/KHDC Radio and KAZU 90.3 FM.

The World Theater is located on the campus of CSU Monterey Bay in Seaside, California.  Tickets for the performances range from $25 to $10.  For more information or to purchase tickets, please call the World Theater box office at (831) 582-4580, 582-5066 TTY/TDD or online at CSUMB.edu/worldtheater.
