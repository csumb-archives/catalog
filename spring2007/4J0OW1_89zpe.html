SON DE MADERA
Thursday, September 21, 2006 • 7:30 P.M.

"The band which takes the folk music it has cherished and brings it into the present, making it sound fresh, modern and fully its own." -- The Los Angeles Times.

Utilizing the rare and beautiful instruments of Eastern Mexico, Son de Madera represents the leading success in the current Son Jarocho movement (a musical and dance genre that blends elements of Spanish melodies and African rhythms).  This talented group from Veracruz, Mexico will transport viewers to a magical place with their unique and sensuous sound, making their concerts appealing to listeners of all ages.

Imani Winds
JOSEPHINE BAKER:  A LIFE OF LE JAZZ HOT
with special guest Rene Marie
Thursday, October 26, 2006 • 7:30 P.M

Josephine Baker is among the most important, vibrant, and controversial African American performers in history.  From her electrifying dance style, to her dedication to the French Underground Resistance, to her compassion for all the world’s children, Ms. Baker lived her life with a fearsome fire.  This multimedia historical performance celebrates the remarkable spirit of this internationally recognized figure and the enduring mark she made as a performer.  To celebrate the 100th Anniversary of Ms. Baker’s birth in 2006, the Grammy-nominated Imani Winds offers this multimedia historical performance.  The evening features special guest Rene Marie.  Directed by Bobbi Leann Williams with choreography by Christopher Huggins.

The Josephine Baker:  A Life of Le Jazz Hot  concert is supported in part by the Seaside Post & Marina Gazette Newspapers.

          

Artwork courtesy of Herbert Barrett Management.

 

...about ANNE
Thursday, November 9, 2006 • 7:30 P.M.

Poet Anne Sexton wrote of the stuff of life: marriage, children, parents, career, obsession, God and godlessness.  Actress Salome Jens lives brilliantly in the words and worlds Sexton created -- those sharp, satiric vignettes of American life, whether in a mental hospital, a suburban kitchen or at the country club dance.  Jens is subtle as well as strong and moves beyond the tormented soul “rowing toward God” to the artist who found Him and made Him in her own words.

Photo:  New Jersey Repertory Theater.

 

DIMI (Women's Sorrow)
by
COMPAGNIE TCHÉTCHÉ
Wednesday, November 29, 2006 • 7:30 P.M.

Compagnie TchéTché is an all-female dance troupe from Abidjan, Côte d’Ivoire.  Founded by Béatrice Kombé in 1997 to “show that woman is not the weaker gender,” TchéTché has emerged as a valiant symbol of the power and passion of women. The Company’s signature style is powerhouse dancing at the edge of physical danger juxtaposed with intense and arresting stillness.  Through dance, the Company reconfigures notions of “subservient” African womanhood into an emphatic reclaiming of power and purpose.  The 2006 North American tour of Dimi is produced by MAPP/MulitArts Projects & Productions.

Funded in part by the National Dance Project of the New England Foundation for the Arts, with lead funding from the Doris Duke Charitable Foundation.  Additional funding provided by The Ford Foundation and The Andrew W. Mellon Foundation.

Photo:  Wolfgang Weiner.

 

THE CLOWN PRINCES
Silent films of Charlie Chaplin, Buster Keaton & Harold Lloyd with the original scores performed by Rick Benjamin’s

PARAGON RAGTIME ORCHESTRA
Thursday, January 25, 2007 • 7:30 P.M.
Location:  Golden State Theatre, Monterey

Regarded as the leading exponent of vintage American popular music, the Paragon Ragtime Orchestra recreates the turn-of-the-century parlor orchestra using authentic period music scores.  Live music will be performed along with film screenings of Buster Keaton’s Cops, Harold Lloyd’s Never Weaken, and Charlie Chaplin’s The Pawn Shop.  Rick Benjamin, founder and director of the orchestra, will present informative introductions to the films and the music written for them.  Join us for an evening of multimedia perfection, the old-fashioned way!  Films subject to change.

“The music is incomparably sweet and stirring.  And Rick Benjamin, who founded and conducts the PRO, is a musician of wit and sensibility.” -- Philadelphia Inquirer.

Photos:  Courtesy of the Paragon Ragtime Orchestra and public domain.

 

 

UNIVERSES, Slanguage
Saturday, February 24, 2006 • 7:30 P.M.

“Their words snake around each other in a seductive portrait…” -- Backstage

The boundary-smashing Slanguage, Universes’ tour-de-force full evening work, brings together the unique mix of language and culture that is life on the American streets. Built loosely around the structure of a subway ride from Brooklyn to the Bronx, Slanguage synthesizes traditional theater with poetry, storytelling, rap, gospel, jazz riffs, bluesy laments, Spanish boleros, and other poetic forms, to paint a rich portrait of the sights and sounds of New York City. Through Slanguage, Universes integrates theatre and street humor with emotional truth, amplifying the issues of poverty, family, drugs, racism, and street violence, yet finding hope in the contemporary urban landscape.

Photo:  Craig Schwartz.

 

NAI-NI CHEN DANCE COMPANY
Wednesday, March 7, 2007 • 7:30 P.M.

The dances of Nai-Ni Chen successfully combine the dynamic freedom of American modern dance with the grace and splendor of Asian Art. The Company’s performances take the audience on an extraordinary artistic journey to places beyond cultural boundaries where tradition meets innovation and freedom arises from discipline. Celebrating the diversity of ideas shaped by the immigrant experience, the Nai-Ni Chen Dance Company bridges the gap of understanding between East and West.

“The viewer is transported into a magical place where movement, mood and music flow together, flooding the sense with one beautiful image after another.” – Asbury Park Press.

Support for the Nai-Ni Chen Dance Company performance made possible in part by The Cannery Row Company.

Photo:  Carol Rosegg.


 

ANDES MANTA
Thursday, May 3, 2007  •  7:30 P.M.

Rooted in the cultural heritage of the Incas and the ancestors the musicians of Andes Manta perform the vibrant music of the Andean mountains of South America. From the lyrical sounds of the quena, or Andean flute, to the haunting tones of six foot long pan pipes, the music of Andes Manta is an experience that never fails to bring audiences to their feet.

Photo:  Courtesy of Andes Manta.
