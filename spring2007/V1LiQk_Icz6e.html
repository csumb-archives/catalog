Monday October 9, 2006
Constitution Day- Agreeing to Disagree: Free Speech, Courtesy, and the Constitution
What:  An important constitutional issue on college campuses, including CSUMB, is how to honor academic freedom and free speech and also practice civil and respectful discourse on subjects that are controversial, emotional, and value laden. It has broad implications in an era of tightened internal security under the Patriot Act and other antiterrorist measures. It has a historical dimension in terms of activist tactics on such issues as antidiscrimination, social justice, and peace. Professor Welsh and Dean Winick will address the legal issues involved in setting acceptable behavioral boundaries of free speech. Professors Feinman and Darling will place this issue in the context of public discourse within the academic community and the broader public arena. Mr. Joseph will provide the perspective of a student journalist on First Amendment questions.
When: October 9, 6:00-7:00 p.m.
Where: University Center Conference Room
For More Information: Contact David Anderson at (831) 582-3818
