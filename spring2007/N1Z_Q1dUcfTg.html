Outcomes, Courses, and Assessment

The program is structured around the working classroom teacher. We offer courses in the evenings, on weekends, during winter session, and throughout the summer. Usually students take two courses per semester.

We also require you to complete a practicum.

Mild/Moderate Level I

The Mild/Moderate Level I and Moderate/Severe Education Specialist Credential programs accent preferred instructional practices to help students with disabilities succeed in school and life. While pursuing your credential, you will acquire the knowledge and skills necessary to satisfy the following California Standards for the Teaching Profession (CSTPs) by successfully completing the listed courses or alternative assessment options.

CSTP 1 Engaging and supporting all students in learning

  • Complete all of the following: ED 609  Math Methods; ED 616 Language & Literacy Development Across the Curriculum I; ED 617 Language & Literacy Development Across the Curriculum II;  SPED 560 Inclusionary Practices; SPED 561 Reading Diagnosis & Preferred Practices for Students with Learning Challenges; SPED 562 Curriculum Development & Preferred  Practices for Students with Learning Challenges; SPED 564  Formal & Informal Assessment for Diverse Student Populations; SPED 565 Supervised Field Experiences in General Education; SPED 566 Behavior Intervention & Support; SPED 567 Practicum/Professional Induction Planning; SPED 568 Methodologies for Culturally & Linguistically Diverse Students with Learning Challenges

CSTP 2 Creating and maintaining effective environments for student learning

  • Complete all of the following: SPED 560 Inclusionary Practices; SPED 562 Curriculum Development & Preferred  Practices for Students with Learning Challenges; SPED 564  Formal & Informal Assessment for Diverse Student Populations; SPED 566 Behavior Intervention & Support; SPED 567 Practicum/Professional Induction Planning

CSTP 3 Understanding and organizing subject matter for student learning

  • Complete all of the following: ED 609  Math Methods; ED 616 Language & Literacy Development Across the Curriculum I; ED 617 Language & Literacy Development Across the Curriculum II;  SPED 560 Inclusionary Practices; SPED 561 Reading Diagnosis & Preferred Practices for Students with Learning Challenges; SPED 564  Formal & Informal Assessment for Diverse Student Populations; SPED 565 Supervised Field Experiences in General Education;  SPED 567 Practicum/Professional Induction Planning; SPED 568 Methodologies for Culturally & Linguistically Diverse Students with Learning Challenges

CSTP 4 Planning instruction and designing learning experiences for all students

  • Complete all of the following: ED 609  Math Methods; ED 616 Language & Literacy Development Across the Curriculum I; ED 617 Language & Literacy Development Across the Curriculum II;  SPED 560 Inclusionary Practices; SPED 561 Reading Diagnosis & Preferred Practices for Students with Learning Challenges; SPED 562 Curriculum Development & Preferred  Practices for Students with Learning Challenges; SPED 564  Formal & Informal Assessment for Diverse Student Populations; SPED 566 Behavior Intervention & Support; SPED 567 Practicum/Professional Induction Planning; SPED 568 Methodologies for Culturally & Linguistically Diverse Students with Learning Challenges

CSTP 5 Assessing student learning

  • Complete all of the following: ED 609  Math Methods; ED 616 Language & Literacy Development Across the Curriculum I; ED 617 Language & Literacy Development Across the Curriculum II;  SPED 560 Inclusionary Practices; SPED 561 Reading Diagnosis & Preferred Practices for Students with Learning Challenges; SPED 562 Curriculum Development & Preferred  Practices for Students with Learning Challenges; SPED 564  Formal & Informal Assessment for Diverse Student Populations; SPED 566 Behavior Intervention & Support; SPED 567 Practicum/Professional Induction Planning; SPED 568 Methodologies for Culturally & Linguistically Diverse Students with Learning Challenges

  • Complete all of the following: ED 616 Language & Literacy Development Across the Curriculum I; ED 617 Language & Literacy Development Across the Curriculum II;  SPED 560 Inclusionary Practices; SPED 561 Reading Diagnosis & Preferred Practices for Students with Learning Challenges; SPED 562 Curriculum Development & Preferred  Practices for Students with Learning Challenges; SPED 564  Formal & Informal Assessment for Diverse Student Populations; SPED 566 Behavior Intervention & Support; SPED 567 Practicum/Professional Induction Planning; SPED 568 Methodologies for Culturally & Linguistically Diverse Students with Learning Challenges
Mild/Moderate Level II
  • Complete all of the following:  SPED 680 Induction Plan/Professional Growth and Assessment (Beginning); SPED 681 ; SPED 681 Advanced Behavioral, Emotional & Environmental Supports for Students with Challenges; SPED 682 Advanced Assessment & Curriculum Instruction for Students with Challenges; SPED 683 Advanced Collaboration & Consultation; SPED 685 Transition & Career Development for the Education Specialist; SPED 689 Induction Plan/Professional Growth for the Education Specialist (Capstone)
  • Complete an Induction Plan with a Special Education faculty member and district support provider.

In consultation with the employer's representative, you may complete up to 25 percent of the Level II program in non-university activities.
