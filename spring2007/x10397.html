WORLD THEATER PSA's
2006/2007 PERFORMING ARTS SERIES

 

SON DE MADERA
Thursday, September 21, 2006, 7:30 P.M.

California State University, Monterey Bay invites you to join us for a performance of Son de Madera on Thursday, September 21, 2006 at 7:30pm at the university’s World Theater.  Tickets range from $25 - $22 and can be purchased by calling the World Theater box office at (831) 582-4580 or online at www.csumb.edu/worldtheater.

Utilizing the rare and beautiful instruments of Eastern Mexico, Son de Madera represents the leading success in the current Son Jarocho movement (a musical and dance genre that blends elements of Spanish melodies and African rhythms).  This talented group from Veracruz, Mexico will transport viewers to a magical place with their unique and sensuous sound, making their concerts appealing to listeners of all ages. 

CSU Monterey Bay’s World Theater is located on Sixth Avenue, on the campus in Seaside, California.

 


JOSEPHINE BAKER:  A LIFE OF LE JAZZ HOT!
Thursday, October 26, 2006, 7:30 P.M.

California State University, Monterey Bay invites you to join us for Josephine Baker: A Life of Le Jazz Hot on Wednesday, October 26, 2005 at 7:30pm at the university’s World Theater.  Tickets range from $25 - $22 and can be purchased by calling the World Theater box office at (831) 582-4580 or online at www.csumb.edu/worldtheater.

Josephine Baker is among the most important, vibrant, and controversial African American performers in history.  From her electrifying dance style, to her dedication to the French Underground Resistance, to her compassion for all the world’s children, Ms. Baker lived her life with a fearsome fire.  This multimedia historical performance celebrates the remarkable spirit of this internationally recognized figure and the enduring mark she made as a performer.  Featuring the Grammy-nominated Imani Winds and jazz singer Rene Marie.

CSU Monterey Bay’s World Theater is located on Sixth Avenue, on the campus in Seaside, California.


 

…about ANNE
Thursday, November 9, 2006, 7:30 P.M.

California State University, Monterey Bay invites you to join us for …about Anne featuring actress Salome Jens on Wednesday, November 9, 2005 at 7:30pm at the university’s World Theater.  Tickets range from $25 - $22 and can be purchased by calling the World Theater box office at (831) 582-4580 or online at www.csumb.edu/worldtheater.

Poet Anne Sexton wrote of the stuff of life: marriage, children, parents, career, obsession, God and godlessness.  Actress Salome Jens lives brilliantly in the words and worlds Sexton created -- those sharp, satiric vignettes of American life, whether in a mental hospital, a suburban kitchen or at the country club dance.  Jens is subtle as well as strong and moves beyond the tormented soul “rowing toward God” to the artist who found Him and made Him in her own words.

CSU Monterey Bay’s World Theater is located on Sixth Avenue, on the campus in Seaside, California.


 

COMPAGNIE TCHÉTCHÉ
Wednesday, November 29, 2006, 7:30 P.M.

California State University, Monterey Bay invites you to join us for international dance sensation Compagnie TchéTché on Wednesday, November 29, 2006 at 7:30pm at the university’s World Theater.  Tickets range from $25 - $22 and can be purchased by calling the World Theater box office at (831) 582-4580 or online at www.csumb.edu/worldtheater.

Compagnie TchéTché is an all-female dance troupe from Abidjan, Côte d’Ivoire. Founded by Béatrice Kombé in 1997 to “show that woman is not the weaker gender,” TchéTché has emerged as a valiant symbol of the power and passion of women. The Company’s signature style is powerhouse dancing at the edge of physical danger juxtaposed with intense and arresting stillness.  Through dance, the Company reconfigures notions of “subservient” African womanhood into an emphatic reclaiming of power and purpose.

CSU Monterey Bay’s World Theater is located on Sixth Avenue, on the campus in Seaside, California.


 

THE CLOWN PRINCES, an evening of silent films and live music by the PARAGON RAGTIME ORCHESTRA
Thursday, January 25, 2007, 7:30 P.M.
 
California State University, Monterey Bay invites you to join us for a performance of The Clown Princes, an evening of live music and silent films at Monterey’s historic Golden State Theatre on Thursday, January 25, 2007 at 7:30pm.  Tickets range from $20 - $15 and can be purchased by calling the World Theater box office at (831) 582-4580 or online at www.csumb.edu/worldtheater.

Regarded as the leading exponent of vintage American popular music, the Paragon Ragtime Orchestra recreates the turn-of-the-century parlor orchestra using authentic period music scores.  Live music will be performed along with film screenings of Buster Keaton’s Cops, Harold Lloyd’s Never Weaken, and Charlie Chaplin’s The Pawn Shop.  Rick Benjamin, founder and director of the orchestra, will present informative introductions to the films and the music written for them.  Join us for an evening of multimedia perfection, the old-fashioned way!  Films subject to change.

CSU Monterey Bay’s World Theater is located on Sixth Avenue, on the campus in Seaside, California.


 

UNIVERSES, Slanguage
Saturday, February 24, 2007, 7:30 P.M.

California State University, Monterey Bay invites you to join us for an evening of poetry and theater featuring Universes on Saturday, February 24, 2007 at 7:30pm at the university’s World Theater.  Tickets range from $25 - $22 and can be purchased by calling the World Theater box office at (831) 582-4580 or online at www.csumb.edu/worldtheater.

The boundary-smashing Slanguage, Universes’ tour-de-force full evening work, brings together the unique mix of language and culture that is life on the American streets. Built loosely around the structure of a subway ride from Brooklyn to the Bronx, Slanguage synthesizes traditional theater with poetry, storytelling, rap, gospel, jazz riffs, bluesy laments, Spanish boleros, and other poetic forms, to paint a rich portrait of the sights and sounds of New York City. Through Slanguage, Universes integrates theatre and street humor with emotional truth, amplifying the issues of poverty, family, drugs, racism, and street violence, yet finding hope in the contemporary urban landscape.

CSU Monterey Bay’s World Theater is located on Sixth Avenue, on the campus in Seaside, California.


 

NAI-NI CHEN DANCE COMPANY
Wednesday, March 7, 2007, 7:30 P.M.

California State University, Monterey Bay invites you to join us for a performance of the Nai-Ni Chen Dance Company on Wednesday, March 7, 2007 at the university’s World Theater.  Tickets range from $25 - $22 and can be purchased by calling the World Theater box office at (831) 582-4580 or online at www.csumb.edu/worldtheater.

The dances of Nai-Ni Chen successfully combine the dynamic freedom of American modern dance with the grace and splendor of Asian Art. The Company’s performances take the audience on an extraordinary artistic journey to places beyond cultural boundaries where tradition meets innovation and freedom arises from discipline. Celebrating the diversity of ideas shaped by the immigrant experience, the Nai-Ni Chen Dance Company bridges the gap of understanding between East and West.

CSU Monterey Bay’s World Theater is located on Sixth Avenue, on the campus in Seaside, California.

 

ANDES MANTA
Thursday, May 3, 2007, 7:30 P.M.

California State University, Monterey Bay invites you to join us for a performance by Andes Manta on Thursday, May 3, 2007 at the university’s World Theater.  Tickets range from $25 - $22 and can be purchased by calling the World Theater box office at (831) 582-4580 or online at www.csumb.edu/worldtheater.

Rooted in the cultural heritage of the Incas, the musicians of Andes Manta perform the vibrant music of the Andean mountains of South America. From the lyrical sounds of the quena, or Andean flute, to the haunting tones of six-foot long pan pipes, the music of Andes Manta is an experience that never fails to bring audiences to their feet.

CSU Monterey Bay’s World Theater is located on Sixth Avenue, on the campus in Seaside, California.
