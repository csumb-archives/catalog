Dental Plans

Dental Plan Options

The university offers eligible employees and their dependents the choice of two dental plans:

  • A "fee for service" indemnity plan in which employees choose their own dentist.
  • A "pre-paid" plan, which requires employees to select dentists from an approved list.

Each plan varies in coverage, so it is essential that you compare plans before making your selection. Collective bargaining agreements designate the level of the dental plan for employees.  Please see the latest Dental Plans Summary for a side by side comparison.  For more information, please visit the Delta Dental website.

Effective 1/1/06, Delta Dental added a new network of providers to our existing “Delta Dental Premier Network” called the “Delta Dental PPO Network”, which may allow you to save even more on your dental care by choosing a dentist who is part of this network.  The dentists in this network are a select group of Delta dentists who have agreed to charge lower fees for their serviced than other dentists.  Generally, you will save 23 – 30% on most services.  Along with your savings, you will be responsible only for your portion of the bill and you avoid having to file claim forms.

Dental Coverage Effective Date

New dental enrollments are effective the first day of the month after the forms are received in UHR. For example, a new staff employee who submits an enrollment to UHR on January 15th will be covered by a dental plan starting February 1st. The 60-day enrollment deadline applies. Processing at the State Controller's Office takes at least 30 days or up to two pay periods.

For academic year faculty hired at the beginning of the fall semester, coverage generally begins October 1st. For faculty hired at the beginning of the spring semester, coverage generally begins March 1st. The 60-day enrollment deadline applies in both instances.

For Delta Dental enrollees, ID cards are not issued to new employees. Your dentist verifies coverage over the phone by calling your plan directly. 

PMI DeltaCare does issue ID cards and will mail them directly to the enrollee.

Dental Employee Cost

CSU currently pays the full premium for the dental plan regardless of how many dependents are covered. Co-pays and deductibles may apply.

Dental Late Enrollment 

If you do not enroll by the 60th calendar day of eligibility, a 90-day waiting period may be applied starting from the day UHR Benefits receives your enrollment form.  The effective date would be the 1st of the month following the completion of the 90-day waiting period.  You may also enroll during the annual open enrollment period.

Dental Plan Changes

An open enrollment period is conducted annually in the fall. At this time employees can change dental plans, and add or delete dependents. Changes are effective on January 1st of the following year. Employees are advised to carefully consider all factors when choosing a dental plan. Plan changes are allowed only during open enrollment. To change your benefit, submit a completed dental enrollment form.

Vision Plan

Vision Plan Details

Currently, a CSU paid vision plan is available to all benefit eligible employees and their dependents administered by Medical Eye Services. Enrollment is automatic. Our group policy number is F21426.

The plan provides the following benefits:

  1. Eye examination - once every 12 months
  2. Standard lenses*/or contact lenses - once every 24 months
  3. Standard frames every 24 months

Employees may use a participating or non-participating provider. However, if you use a non-participating provider, your benefits will be paid at a reduced rate. Participating provider information can be found on the Blue Shield/ Medical Eye Services website.

To use your vision plan, bring a vision claim form to your eye appointment. If you are unable to download the forms, they may also be obtained from the Campus Service Center. Employees are encouraged to call the customer service number on the claim form to verify eligibility for services. If you use a non-participating provider, you will be asked to pay for the services up front. Requests for reimbursement must be submitted within the timeline specified by the carrier.

Video Display Terminal Glasses   

All active employees (dependents are not covered) who utilize a video display terminal four or more hours per day on a regular basis are eligible for this benefit.  In order to take advantage of this option, please bring the VDT claim form with you to your eye appointment.

Vision Coverage Effective Date

The effective date is generally the first of the month following the employee's start date.
