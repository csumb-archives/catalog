Goals

  • To promote a healthy lifestyle by getting active and relieving stress, encompassed in an atmosphere of friendly competition.
  • To challenge participants to create or go above and beyond their existing personal walking goals each week.
  • To work with and encourage team members in their personal fitness goals.
  • To have fun!

[TOP]

What you need to participate

  • A team of 5 people made up of CSUMB Staff & Faculty with one person named the team captain**
  • Team name
  • One pedometer per person

** Team Captain Responsibilities: Team Captains must be available for a brief meeting to go over rules and logistical information.  The meeting will be Friday, 9/22/06 at 11am, bld. 29, room 116.   Likewise, team captains will be responsible to collect their individual team member's weekly steps log every Monday following the initial week and turn them in to UHR by 5pm that day. 

 

[TOP]

 

Duration

The one-month challenge begins 9/25/06 and ends 10/20/06.

Rules of the Challenge

With a pedometer, each team member will fill out a Weekly Steps Log, tracking how many steps they take in a 24-hour period during business days (Monday – Friday only).  Each team member will be on the honor system when tracking his or her steps.  Every Monday following the initial week, the team captain will collect the team member’s tracking sheets and turn them into UHR by 5pm.  Forms received after the deadline will not be counted. Team scores will be tabulated and posted weekly on this website.

 

The team with the highest number of steps will be deemed the “Workplace Walk-off Challenge Champions” and will win the Golden Shoe Trophy, to be displayed in the workplace and shared amongst the teammates until the following year’s challenge begins.

Tips to Increase your Steps

The Workplace Walk-off Challenge is not meant to impede your workday but enhance it.  Following are some tips to obtain steps while accomplishing your tasks at work:

 

For the Workplace:

  • Wear your pedometer throughout your day to track all your steps.
  • Park further away.
  • Use the furthest entrance into your workplace from your parking spot
  • Walk to meetings or have “walking meetings.”
  • Walk during your 10-minute breaks.
  • For every hour of work, take a 1-2 minute stretch break, walking throughout your building.
  • Circle the room when waiting for meetings to start.
  • Use the restroom, copy machine, printer, water fountain, break room, etc. that is further from your work area.
  • Take the stairs rather than the elevator, especially for one to three floors, both up and down.
  • When making a phone call, stand up and pace around as you talk.
  • Rather than phone or email, walk to a coworker's office and talk to them live.
  • Form a workplace walking group and walk together during lunch or break.
  • When traveling and waiting at the airport, secure your bags and take a good walk around the terminal area. Don't take the people-mover sidewalks.

At Home:

  • Hide the TV remote and walk to the TV to change channels.
  • During TV commercials, get up and walk around the house.
  • When doing errands, park in a central location and walk to your store destinations.
  • Return the shopping cart all the way into the store after grocery shopping.
  • Skip the drive through window - get out, park and walk into the bank or fast food stop instead.
  • If you take your kids to sports or activities, dedicate 10-20 minutes of that time to walking around after dropping them off or when you arrive early to pick them up.
  • When talking on the phone to that special friend or relative, walk throughout your house.
  • Don't stand, pace - when waiting for the bus, waiting at an elevator, the doctor’s office, your dinner reservation at your favorite restaurant, etc. pace rather than just sitting or standing. (People may think you’re crazy, but you’ll accumulate more steps!)

Family or Friend outings:

  • Form a neighborhood walking group and walk together in the mornings or after work.
  • Make a family habit of taking a 10-20 minute walk after dinner together, or first thing in the morning.
  • Visit local parks and enjoy the trails. Set a goal to visit them all in the next couple of months.
  • Take advantage of seasonal walks: Holiday lights, flowers and gardens, nature trails, local festivals, historic site tours, autumn leaves.

Walking Steps Equivalents
1 mile = 2100 average steps
1 block = 200 average steps
10 minutes of walking = 1200 steps on average
Bicycling or swimming = 150 steps for each minute.
Weight lifting = 100 steps per minute
Roller-skating = 200 steps per minute
