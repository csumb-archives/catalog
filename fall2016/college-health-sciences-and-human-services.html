College of Health Sciences and Human Services

Mission

Strengthening the quality of life of the diverse communities with whom we live, work and serve through collaborative and community-informed education, research, training and service.

Dean & staff

Dr. Britt Rios-Ellis has served as the founding Dean of the College of Health Sciences and Human Services since August of 2014. Since her arrival she has worked with faculty and community leaders to establish a college vision, mission, and guiding principles. She is committed to ensuring that the College becomes the health science and human services workforce development hub of the region. Diversity and cultural relevance are high priorities for Dean Rios-Ellis and faculty, and college programming and curriculum undergird this fundamental principle.

Dr. Rios-Ellis came from CSU Long Beach where she served as a Professor of Health Science and Founding Director of the National Council of La Raza (NCLR)/CSULB Center for Latino Community Health, Evaluation and Leadership Training and Centro Salud es Cultura. She raised over 55 million in federal and private grants and has directed several Latino health-related projects, as well efforts focused on increasing underrepresented minorities in the biomedical and behavioral sciences.

Dr. Rios-Ellis has served as a consultant and a commissioned member for several organizations including: Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, the Health Resources Services Administration, the Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration, the Pan American Health Organization, the National Association for Community Health Centers, the National Minority AIDS Education and Training Center, and the US Office of Minority Health.

She earned a bachelor’s degree in Political Science and Spanish, a Master of Science in Health and Fitness Management and a Doctoral Degree in Community Health, all from the University of Oregon.
