AVP for University Personnel

CSUMB became a higher education leader in integrating human resources functions for staff and academic personnel in October 2011. In July 2015, Natalie King became the Associate Vice President (AVP) for Human Resources and Academic Personnel, reporting directly to the President. King is an attorney with substantial experience in higher education, employment and labor law. She earned her Juris Doctor degree from Marquette University, and is a member of the state bars in both California and Wisconsin. King earned her undergraduate degree in English from the University of Iowa. She has also been a faculty member at the University of Wisconsin Law School, William Mitchell Law School, Santa Clara University Law School and San José State, teaching courses in employment law, legal analysis, and appellate advocacy. Prior to her employment at CSUMB, King served as the Assistant Associate Vice President for Faculty Affairs at San José State University where she was responsible for the administration of two academic collective bargaining agreements, oversight of employment issues regarding faculty personnel, and training faculty, chairs, directors, and academic administrators.
