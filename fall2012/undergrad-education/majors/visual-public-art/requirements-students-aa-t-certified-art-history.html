If you transferred into CSUMB as an AA-T-certified student in Art History, you must complete the following courses for your bachelor's degree in Visual and Public Art at CSUMB. If you are unsure about your transfer status, please talk to an advisor. 

Core Curriculum (30 units)

VPA 300: VPA Major ProSeminar

VPA 302: VPA Visiting Artist Series (twice)

VPA 307: Diverse Hist Contemp Art

VPA 308: Ways Of Seeing  (C1 Upper Division GE)

VPA 309: Materials & Methods

VPA 310: Mixed Media Drawing

VPA 405: Senior Capstone (2 semesters, Senior year)

Suggested Courses for Major Learning Outcomes (16 units)

Choose one for MLO 1: VPA 305: Media Culture; VPA 313: Regional Art History; VPA 341: Feminist Art: History, Theory; VPA 347: Chicana/o-Black Art

Choose VPA 310: Mixed Media Drawing (which also meets core curriculum) and one other for MLO 2: VPA 301: Beginning Painting; VPA 303: Beginning Sculpture; VPA 304: Painting & Mural; VPA 305: Media Culture; VPA 315: Life Drawing; VPA 316: Photography as Art AND VPA 316L: Photography as Art Lab; VPA 326: Photo as Art II: Alternative and VPA 326L: Photo as Art II: Alternative; VPA 330: Tile & Mosaic in Public Art; VPA 401: Installation & Performance Art; VPA 403: Sculpture & 3D Design

Choose one for MLO 3 and MLO 4: VPA 306S: Digital Public Art or VPA 320S: Museum Studies (D4 Upper Division GE Service Learning)

Choose for MLO 5: VPA 309: Materials & Methods (which also meets core curriculum)

Chooe one for MLO 6 and MLO 7: VPA 312: Vis Arts Ed For Teachers or VPA 321: Museum Collections Practices

University Requirements (12 units)

Any upper divison D2 course

Two C3 courses

Elective (2 units)

Choose any course
