Community Health Requirements

  • View by concentration or emphasis:
  • Public Administration Emphasis

  • Community Health
Credits Required: 

Total credits: 120

Credits at CSUMB: 70

Credits in the major: 70

You may declare CHHS as your major at any time during your undergraduate studies at CSUMB. However, we recommend that you declare as early as possible so that you can begin lower-division prerequisite and recommended courses. You officially begin the major by enrolling in CHHS 300: Major ProSeminar, where you work with the instructor to create or revise your Individual Learning Plan (ILP). The ILP maps out how you will achieve each of the Major Learning Outcomes designated below by successfully completing the listed courses or alternative assessment options. All CHHS majors will choose one of the following concentrations: 1) Social Work Concentration, 2) Community Health Concentration, or 3) the Public Administration Emphasis. Students interested in pursuing more than one concentration or emphasis should meet with a CHHS faculty advisor to develop an ILP.  You conclude the major by completing your CHHS field-based Capstone, a publicly presented project that displays your cumulative learning in the major.

Major Requirements and Learning Outcomes are identified below. In order to graduate, students must also meet all general education and university requirements. 

MLO 1 Collaboration (C)

Demonstrate the ability to work in teams in interprofessional settings across traditional lines of programs, agencies, disciplines and diverse communities to establish common missions and purposes, and to collaborate with others in decision making, learning, completing tasks and applying knowledge of group process and group interaction.

Required Courses

  • All CHHS majors must complete the following core course:
  • CHHS 330: Collab Community Leadership (4 units)

MLO 2 Conflict Resolution, Negotiation and Mediation (CR/N)

Demonstrate the ability to understand the dynamics of human negotiation among conflicting interest groups and how to achieve mutual agreement.

Required Courses

  • All CHHS majors must complete the following core course:
  • CHHS 340: Conflict Res/Collab Negotia (4 units)

MLO 3 Cross-Cultural Competency (CCC)

Demonstrate the ability to be comfortable with differences between self and others, to engage in a process characterized by mutual respect and sensitivity, to assess the needs and capabilities of culturally diverse populations and to communicate effectively across cultural groups to deliver appropriate health and human services.

Required Courses

  • Community Health Concentration
  • All students in the Community Health concentration must complete both of the following courses:
  • PH 407: Multicultural Health Educatn (4 units)
  • PH 417: Global Women's Health Issues (4 units)

MLO 4 Financial Management (FM)

Demonstrate the ability to understand the budgeting process and the financial management of public agencies and nonprofit organizations.

Required Courses

  • All CHHS majors must complete the following two core courses:
  • CHHS 435: Intro Nonprofit/Public Admin (4 units)
  • CHHS 445: Principles of Grantsmanship (2 units)

MLO 5 Information Management (IM)

Demonstrate the ability to apply computer and Internet technology to health and human services practice and public policy development.

Required Courses

  • All CHHS majors must complete the following core courses:
  • CHHS 300: Major ProSeminar (4 units)
  • CHHS 385L: Research Methods Lab (1 unit)
  • CHHS 435: Intro Nonprofit/Public Admin (4 units)

MLO 6 Knowledge of Health and Human Services (KHHS)

Demonstrate an understanding of the fundamental principles and issues common to the major fields of health and human services including community health, social welfare and public policy; and demonstrate the knowledge, skills and abilities pertinent to the health and/or human service concentration area(s) selected.

Required Courses

  • All CHHS majors must complete the following core courses:
  • SW 310: Introduction to Social Work (4 units)
  • PH 320: Intro to Community Health (4 units)
  • CHHS 396S: Jr Field Pract Seminar SL (3 units)
  • CHHS 496A: Sr Field Pract Seminar-A (3 units)
  • CHHS 496B: Sr Field Pract Seminar-B (3 units)
  • Community Health Concentration
  • In addition to the core courses listed above, students in the Community Health concentration also complete:
  • PH 407: Multicultural Health Educatn (4 units)

MLO 7 Leadership (L)

Demonstrate an understanding of and begin to master the skills to motivate others by promoting a shared vision and setting clear direction for collaborative implementation of sustained change in interprofessional settings and diverse communities.

Required Courses

  • All CHHS majors must complete the following core course:
  • CHHS 330: Collab Community Leadership (4 units)

MLO 8 Professional Communication (PC)

Demonstrate the ability to initiate and maintain a flow of information between senders and receivers through the use of critical listening, and oral and written communication skills; communicate basic and complex ideas to a variety of groups; appreciate the organizational and cultural contexts within which communication occurs; and conduct professional quality oral and electronic presentations.

Required Courses

  • All CHHS majors must complete the following core courses:
  • CHHS 302: Professional Communication (4 units)
  • CHHS 400A: Senior Capstone-A (4 units)
  • CHHS 400B: Senior Capstone-B (4 units)
